**Purpose:** Validate high-ticket offer structure before presenting to prospects
**Standard:** Ryan Deiss CVO Profit Maximizer + Cialdini Authority & Social Proof
**Price Range:** $1,000 – $50,000+
**Run:** Before presenting high-ticket offer to any prospect

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Offer Structure

### Value Justification
- [ ] The result the prospect achieves is worth significantly more than the price
- [ ] The value-to-price ratio is demonstrable (not just claimed):
  - ROI calculation shows [price] generates [X × price] in value
  - Alternative cost comparison shows: doing this themselves costs [more]
  - Time-savings calculation: [price] saves [X hours at $Y/hour = $Z]
- [ ] Value justification is stated explicitly in the sales process

### Transformation Clarity
- [ ] The "before" state is precisely defined (where they are now)
- [ ] The "after" state is precisely defined (where they'll be after)
- [ ] The transformation is specific, measurable, and time-bound
- [ ] The transformation is something the prospect desperately wants

**Complete the transformation statement:**
> "You go from [specific before state] to [specific after state] in [timeframe]."


### Delivery Confidence
- [ ] The team can reliably deliver the promised transformation
- [ ] Success criteria are defined and measurable
- [ ] A track record of client results exists to prove delivery capability
- [ ] Refund/guarantee policy is defined and fair

---

## 🟡 HIGH PRIORITY — Authority & Proof

### Authority Establishment (Cialdini)
- [ ] Seller's credentials and expertise are documented and verifiable
- [ ] Specific results achieved for past clients are documented
- [ ] Third-party validation present (media, awards, associations)
- [ ] Case studies available with specific numbers, not just testimonials

**Case Study Format:**
```
[Client Name/Role/Company]
Before: [Specific situation and metric]
Process: [What was done — briefly]
After: [Specific result with number] in [timeframe]
Quote: "[Direct quote about experience]"
```

### Social Proof Stack
- [ ] Minimum 3 case studies available (not just testimonials)
- [ ] Case studies represent the target buyer profile
- [ ] At least one result is dramatically impressive (the "hero case study")
- [ ] Video testimonials available if possible

### Risk Reversal
- [ ] A clear guarantee or performance promise is offered
- [ ] Guarantee is specific (not vague "satisfaction guarantee")
- [ ] Terms are clear (what triggers the guarantee, how to claim it)
- [ ] Guarantee removes the primary objection to purchasing

---

## 🟢 BEST PRACTICE — Sales Process

### Qualification Criteria
- [ ] Qualifying criteria defined (who is a good fit, who is not)
- [ ] Disqualification criteria defined (who to turn away)
- [ ] Application or discovery call process requires prospect investment
- [ ] High-ticket is only presented to qualified prospects

### The Application/Discovery Call
- [ ] Discovery call agenda defined
- [ ] Pre-call homework assigned to prospect (increases commitment)
- [ ] Call focused on THEIR situation, not on the offer features
- [ ] Close happens on the call, not via follow-up email

### Scarcity/Access
- [ ] Limited spots available (real capacity constraint)
- [ ] Waitlist or application process creates perceived exclusivity
- [ ] Prospect must qualify — they can't just buy (increases desirability)

---

## ❌ AUTO-FAIL CONDITIONS

- [ ] ❌ Price presented before value is established
- [ ] ❌ No case studies — only vague testimonials
- [ ] ❌ Transformation promise is vague ("you'll do better in business")
- [ ] ❌ No guarantee of any kind (maximum risk on buyer)
- [ ] ❌ Anyone can buy without qualification process
- [ ] ❌ Sales process is email-only (no voice/video call for $1,000+)
- [ ] ❌ Authority is only self-claimed, not third-party validated
