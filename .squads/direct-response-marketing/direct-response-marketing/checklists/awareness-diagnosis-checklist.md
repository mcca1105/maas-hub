## 🔴 CRITICAL — Complete Before Routing to Any Specialist

### Step 1: Traffic Source Classification
- [ ] Traffic source identified: ___________________
- [ ] Cold traffic (Facebook Ads, YouTube Ads, Display, Cold Email) → likely Stage 1-2
- [ ] Warm traffic (Google Search generic, Retargeting, Referral) → likely Stage 2-3
- [ ] Hot traffic (Buyer list, Email subscriber, Brand search) → likely Stage 4-5
- [ ] Traffic temperature recorded: **Cold / Warm / Hot**

### Step 2: Problem Awareness Test
Ask: "Does the prospect know they have the specific problem this product solves?"
- [ ] NO → **Stage 1 (Unaware)** — STOP here
- [ ] YES → Continue to Step 3

### Step 3: Solution Awareness Test
Ask: "Does the prospect know that solutions to their problem exist?"
- [ ] NO → **Stage 2 (Problem Aware)** — STOP here
- [ ] YES → Continue to Step 4

### Step 4: Product Category Awareness Test
Ask: "Does the prospect know that products like this exist?"
- [ ] NO → **Stage 3 (Solution Aware)** — STOP here
- [ ] YES → Continue to Step 5

### Step 5: Brand Awareness Test
Ask: "Has the prospect been exposed to THIS specific product before?"
- [ ] NO → **Stage 4 (Product Aware)** — STOP here
- [ ] YES, and ready to buy → **Stage 5 (Most Aware)**

**CONFIRMED STAGE:** _______

## 🟡 HIGH PRIORITY — Validate and Document

### Market Sophistication Assessment
- [ ] Research how long this market/product category has existed
- [ ] Count the number of competing products with similar claims
- [ ] Identify the dominant mechanism already in the market

**Sophistication Level:**
- [ ] Level 1 — First product of its kind. State the claim directly.
- [ ] Level 2 — Competitors exist. Amplify the claim.
- [ ] Level 3 — All claims amplified. Introduce unique mechanism.
- [ ] Level 4 — Mechanisms commoditized. Identity/tribe positioning required.
- [ ] Level 5 — Full saturation. Pioneer new sub-market.

**Confirmed Level:** _______

### VOC Validation
- [ ] At least 3 VOC sources reviewed (Amazon, Reddit, forums, surveys)
- [ ] Prospect language matches diagnosed awareness stage
- [ ] Dominant emotion identified: **Fear / Frustration / Hope / Curiosity / Urgency**
- [ ] Primary desire confirmed (what they most want)

---

## 🟢 BEST PRACTICE — Copy Strategy Alignment

### Stage-Specific Copy Requirements

**If Stage 1 (Unaware):**
- [ ] Opening uses pattern interrupt or shocking revelation
- [ ] Product name NOT in headline
- [ ] Problem introduced as hidden/unknown
- [ ] Story or news format selected

**If Stage 2 (Problem Aware):**
- [ ] Opening validates their frustration
- [ ] VOC language used in first paragraph
- [ ] Copy frames solution as "new approach" not just "solution"
- [ ] Empathy established before solution introduced

**If Stage 3 (Solution Aware):**
- [ ] Unique mechanism identified and named
- [ ] Opening compares to solutions they've already tried
- [ ] Mechanism explains why previous solutions failed
- [ ] Problem setup kept short (they know the problem)

**If Stage 4 (Product Aware):**
- [ ] Top objections identified and addressed
- [ ] Proof stack heavy (testimonials, data, before/after)
- [ ] Offer sharpened (bonuses, guarantee prominent)
- [ ] Comparison to alternatives available

**If Stage 5 (Most Aware):**
- [ ] Headline leads with offer or deal
- [ ] Price appears early
- [ ] Urgency/scarcity prominent
- [ ] Copy is short — they know everything already

---

## ❌ AUTO-FAIL CONDITIONS

- [ ] ❌ Copy written before awareness stage confirmed
- [ ] ❌ Cold traffic directed to Stage 4-5 copy (assumes knowledge they don't have)
- [ ] ❌ Stage 1 copy used for email list (warm audience — over-educates)
- [ ] ❌ Product name in headline for Stage 1-2 (kills curiosity)
- [ ] ❌ Mechanism skipped for Stage 3 audience (critical gap)
