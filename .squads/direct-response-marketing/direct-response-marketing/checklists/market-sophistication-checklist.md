# Market Sophistication Checklist
**Agent:** `@drm:eugene-schwartz`
**Purpose:** Determine market sophistication level to select correct copy strategy
**Standard:** Eugene Schwartz — Breakthrough Advertising, Market Sophistication Framework
**Run:** At the start of any copy project, alongside awareness stage diagnosis

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Sophistication Level Diagnosis

### Level Identification Questions

**Question 1:** Is this the FIRST product of its kind on the market?
- [ ] YES → **Level 1** — State the claim directly. The market has never heard anything like this.

**Question 2:** Do competitors already exist with similar claims?
- [ ] YES → Continue to Q3

**Question 3:** Have competitors already AMPLIFIED their claims (bigger, faster, stronger)?
- [ ] YES → Continue to Q4

**Question 4:** Have multiple competitors introduced unique mechanisms?
- [ ] YES → Continue to Q5

**Question 5:** Has the market seen so many mechanisms that even mechanisms are commoditized?
- [ ] YES → **Level 5** — Pioneer a new sub-market or radical identity positioning

---

## 🟡 HIGH PRIORITY — Level-Specific Copy Strategy

### Level 1 — First to Market
**Copy rule:** State the claim. The market has never heard it.
- [ ] Headline leads with the claim directly
- [ ] No need for a complex mechanism (the claim IS the mechanism)
- [ ] No need to overcome sophistication ("other products fail because...")
- [ ] Keep it simple and direct

**Example pattern:** "[Product] [result] — [Simple direct claim]"

### Level 2 — Growing Competition
**Copy rule:** Amplify. Make the claim bigger, faster, stronger.
- [ ] Headline amplifies the dominant market claim
- [ ] Use superlatives strategically ("the most powerful," "the fastest way")
- [ ] Specificity beats amplitude ("lose 22 lbs" > "lose weight fast")
- [ ] Still don't need a complex mechanism

**Example pattern:** "Now you can [result] [better/faster/easier] than ever before"

### Level 3 — Saturated Claims (MOST COMMON STAGE)
**Copy rule:** Introduce a unique mechanism. Re-frame the problem.
- [ ] Headline leads with the new/unique mechanism, not the claim
- [ ] Mechanism explains why old solutions fail
- [ ] Mechanism names a hidden root cause the prospect hasn't heard about
- [ ] Copy format: "Finally, a new [mechanism] that [result] because [unique reason]"

**Diagnosis: "Am I at Level 3?"**
- [ ] Every competitor makes the same basic claim (everyone says "lose weight fast")
- [ ] Prospect has seen 5+ similar ads before (jaded about claims)
- [ ] Standard benefit headlines are NOT working in this market

### Level 4 — Mechanisms Commoditized
**Copy rule:** Associate mechanism with identity, tribe, or worldview.
- [ ] Headline speaks to who the prospect IS, not what they get
- [ ] Copy creates a tribe ("this is for people who...")
- [ ] Product becomes an expression of their identity, not just a solution
- [ ] Mechanism is still present but secondary to identity positioning

**Example pattern:** "For [Identity] who refuse to accept [Status Quo]"

### Level 5 — Full Market Saturation
**Copy rule:** Pioneer a new sub-market entirely.
- [ ] Identify an underserved segment within the market
- [ ] Position the product as specifically built FOR that segment
- [ ] Open a new conversation the existing market isn't having
- [ ] Alternatively: radical authenticity/transparency (going against the grain)

**Example pattern:** Pivot to an entirely different avatar, problem frame, or solution category

## 🟢 BEST PRACTICE — Sophistication Verification

### Market Research to Confirm Level
- [ ] Google Ads search for top keywords in the niche — how many advertisers?
  - 1-5 advertisers: Likely Level 1-2
  - 5-20 advertisers: Likely Level 2-3
  - 20+ advertisers: Likely Level 3-5
- [ ] Review top 5 competitor headlines — are they all saying the same thing?
  - Yes → Level 3+ (mechanism required)
  - No → Level 1-2 (differentiation available through claims alone)
- [ ] Ask: "If I ran this headline 3 years ago, would it have converted better?"
  - YES → The market has gotten more sophisticated. Level up the strategy.

### The Saturation Test
- [ ] Does the typical prospect in this market roll their eyes at standard claims?
- [ ] Have they seen 10+ ads for similar products in the last 90 days?
- [ ] Do they say things like "all of these products are the same"?
- [ ] All YES → Level 4-5 strategy required

---

## ❌ AUTO-FAIL CONDITIONS

- [ ] ❌ Using Level 1 copy (direct claim) in a Level 3-5 market (will be ignored)
- [ ] ❌ Introducing a mechanism in a Level 1 market (over-complex, confusing)
- [ ] ❌ Identity positioning used in Level 1-2 market (audience isn't jaded enough)
- [ ] ❌ Sophistication level assumed without researching competitors first
