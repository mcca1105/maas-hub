# Email Subject Line Checklist
**Agent:** `@drm:gary-halbert` / `@drm:frank-kern`
**Purpose:** Validate email subject lines before sending to list
**Standard:** Halbert A-Pile principle + Kern Intent-Based Branding
**Run:** Before every email broadcast or sequence step

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Open Rate Foundation

### The A-Pile Test (Halbert)
A subject line passes if it looks like personal mail, not promotional mail.
- [ ] Subject line could have been written by a person, not a company
- [ ] Does NOT contain: "% Off", "Sale", "Buy Now", "Discount", "Free" (if cold list)
- [ ] Does NOT start with the company/brand name
- [ ] Could this subject line appear in the prospect's personal inbox from a friend?

### Clarity vs Curiosity Selection
- [ ] For cold/new subscribers → **Curiosity**: create an information gap
- [ ] For warm buyers → **Clarity**: state the benefit directly
- [ ] Strategy selected: **Curiosity / Clarity / Hybrid**

### Character Count
- [ ] Subject line is under 50 characters (ideal for mobile preview)
- [ ] Critical words appear in first 30 characters
- [ ] Preview text (snippet) supports and expands on subject line

---

## 🟡 HIGH PRIORITY — Subject Line Types

### Curiosity Subject Lines (Best for Cold Traffic)
Verify one of these patterns is present:
- [ ] Incomplete statement that demands completion: "The reason most [X] fail (it's not what you think)"
- [ ] Personal story tease: "What happened when I tried [unusual thing]"
- [ ] Numbered revelation: "3 things your [authority figure] won't tell you about [topic]"
- [ ] Pattern interrupt: "Don't [common advice] (here's why)"
- [ ] Question with implied surprising answer: "Is [common practice] actually hurting you?"

### Benefit Subject Lines (Best for Warm/Hot Traffic)
Verify specificity:
- [ ] Contains a specific number or result ("Lost 23 lbs in 6 weeks")
- [ ] Names the timeframe ("[Result] by [specific date/timeframe]")
- [ ] Names the mechanism briefly if prospect knows it

### Personal/Story Subject Lines (Best for Engagement Sequences)
- [ ] Feels like it's from one person to one person
- [ ] References a shared experience or belief
- [ ] Includes a natural conversational tone ("Quick question", "Real talk:")

---

## 🟢 BEST PRACTICE — Deliverability & Testing

### Spam Filter Check
Run subject line through deliverability scanner before sending.
- [ ] No all-caps words
- [ ] No excessive punctuation (!!!, ???, ...)
- [ ] No spam trigger words: guaranteed, winner, free offer, click here, earn money
- [ ] No excessive symbols ($$$, ***) unless strategic

### A/B Testing
- [ ] Subject line A/B test set up (minimum 20% of list per variant)
- [ ] Win condition defined (open rate threshold to determine winner)
- [ ] Test runs minimum 4 hours before winner selected
- [ ] Winner documented in swipe file if open rate exceeds 30%

### Segmentation Match
- [ ] Subject line matches the segment receiving it
- [ ] Buyer list subject lines differ from prospect list
- [ ] Re-engagement subject lines flag clearly ("We miss you" style for dormant)

---

## ❌ AUTO-FAIL CONDITIONS

- [ ] ❌ Subject line starts with company name (promotes, doesn't intrigue)
- [ ] ❌ Contains "RE:" when it's not actually a reply (deceptive)
- [ ] ❌ Uses clickbait that the email body doesn't deliver on (destroys trust)
- [ ] ❌ Duplicate subject line sent to same list within 30 days
- [ ] ❌ Subject line has no connection to the email body content
- [ ] ❌ Excessive capitalization: "THIS IS THE BEST DEAL EVER"

## SUBJECT LINE SWIPE BANK

High-performing patterns to adapt:
```
Curiosity:
- "I almost didn't share this..."
- "The [X] nobody talks about"
- "Weird [result] trick actually works"
- "You're probably doing [X] wrong"
- "[First name], did you see this?"

Urgency:
- "Last chance (tonight at midnight)"
- "Opens in 2 hours [deadline]"
- "[X] spots left"

Personal:
- "Quick question for you"
- "Be honest with me..."
- "Can I ask you something?"

Benefit (warm):
- "[X] results in [Y] days"
- "How [Name] [specific result]"
- "The [timeframe] [result] plan"
```
