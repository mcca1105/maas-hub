# Sales Letter Review Checklist
**Agent:** @copy-legend
**Purpose:** Pre-flight validation before publishing
**Standard:** Eugene Schwartz + Gary Halbert principles

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL (MUST PASS)

### Research & Foundation
- [ ] **80% time on research** (Halbert: "Copy is research assembled")
- [ ] **Amazon reviews mined** (1-3 star for pain, 5-star for language)
- [ ] **Reddit/forum research done** (exact customer phrases documented)
- [ ] **Awareness stage identified** (Unaware, Problem, Solution, Product, Most Aware)
- [ ] **Market sophistication determined** (Stage 1-5)

### Headline (Schwartz: "80% of selling happens here")
- [ ] **10+ variations tested** (never settle for first idea)
- [ ] **Promise specific result** (not vague)
- [ ] **Creates curiosity** (open loop or contradiction)
- [ ] **Qualifies audience** (for [who])
- [ ] **Under 20 words** (scannable)

### Core Structure
- [ ] **Pre-head present** (credibility/qualifier)
- [ ] **Deck copy clarifies** (expands headline promise)
- [ ] **Lead hooks in 100 words** (Halbert: "First 100 words are critical")
- [ ] **Story is relatable** (prospect sees themselves)
- [ ] **Bullets create curiosity** (fascination bullets - 15-20)

### Proof Elements
- [ ] **Social proof every 500 words** (testimonials, data, case studies)
- [ ] **Testimonials have specifics** (not "this is great" but "I made $X in Y days")
- [ ] **Authority signals present** (credentials, media, endorsements)
- [ ] **Video testimonials included** (if possible - 3x trust)

### Offer & Guarantee
- [ ] **Value stack visible** (each component priced)
- [ ] **Total value shown** (sum of all components)
- [ ] **Price reveal strategic** (after value established)
- [ ] **Guarantee is STRONG** (60-90 day better-than-risk-free)
- [ ] **Bonuses add value** (not just fluff)

### CTAs
- [ ] **CTA every 700 words** (Schwartz: "Multiple chances to buy")
- [ ] **CTAs benefit-focused** ("Get instant access" not "Submit")
- [ ] **Urgency/scarcity present** (deadline, limited spots)
- [ ] **P.S. included** ⚠️ **CRITICAL** (many skip to this - recap main benefit)

---

## 🟡 HIGH PRIORITY

### Copy Quality
- [ ] **You-focused** (90%+ "you" vs "we/I")
- [ ] **Benefits, not features** (what they GET, not what IT HAS)
- [ ] **Specific numbers** (37% not "significant", $2,473 not "a lot")
- [ ] **Active voice** (you will GET vs you can receive)
- [ ] **Scannable format** (bullets, subheads, bold, short paragraphs)

### Awareness Stage Match
**Stage 1 (Unaware):**
- [ ] Problem education comes first (don't sell yet)
- [ ] Agitate the problem (make them feel it)

**Stage 2 (Problem Aware):**
- [ ] Solution category introduced (mechanism type)
- [ ] Differentiation from "old ways"

**Stage 3 (Solution Aware):**
- [ ] Unique mechanism highlighted (why THIS solution)
- [ ] Superiority demonstrated (vs competition)

**Stage 4 (Product Aware):**
- [ ] Objections addressed (FAQ section)
- [ ] Fit demonstrated (this is for YOU)

**Stage 5 (Most Aware):**
- [ ] Urgency maximized (deadline, scarcity)
- [ ] Easy purchase process (remove friction)

### Fascination Bullets (Gary Halbert Style)
- [ ] **Curiosity gap created** (tease without revealing)
- [ ] **Specificity used** (page numbers, exact details)
- [ ] **Benefits promised** (what they'll discover)
- [ ] **15-20 bullets minimum** (throughout letter)

**Examples:**
- ✅ "The 'backwards' technique on page 47 that melts fat while you sleep..."
- ✅ "Why eating MORE of this 'forbidden' food accelerates weight loss (surprising study on page 23)..."
- ❌ "Tips for losing weight" (boring, no curiosity)

---

## 🟢 BEST PRACTICE

### Advanced Techniques
- [ ] **Starving crowd addressed** (Halbert: right audience > everything)
- [ ] **PAS formula used** (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
- [ ] **Contrast creates desire** (before vs after, old way vs new way)
- [ ] **Future pacing** (imagine yourself with result)
- [ ] **Open loops** (questions answered later)

### Length & Format
- [ ] **3,000-10,000 words** (long-form for complex offers)
- [ ] **Paragraphs 2-3 sentences max** (white space = readability)
- [ ] **Subheads every 300 words** (guide scanning)
- [ ] **Bold key phrases** (highlight important points)
- [ ] **Mobile-optimized** (readable on phone)

---

## ❌ COMMON MISTAKES (Auto-Fail)

### Research Failures
- [ ] ❌ **Writing before researching** (violates Schwartz principle)
- [ ] ❌ **Generic language** (not using customer words)
- [ ] ❌ **Wrong awareness stage** (selling to unaware = fail)

### Headline Failures
- [ ] ❌ **Weak headline** (generic, no curiosity, vague)
- [ ] ❌ **Didn't test 10+** (Halbert: "Headlines are 80% of selling")
- [ ] ❌ **Too long** (>25 words = loses punch)

### Body Failures
- [ ] ❌ **Features instead of benefits** (talking about product vs transformation)
- [ ] ❌ **We-focused** (talking about company vs customer)
- [ ] ❌ **No bullets** (or boring bullets)
- [ ] ❌ **No social proof** (or buried at bottom)

### Offer Failures
- [ ] ❌ **No value stack** (just price = weak)
- [ ] ❌ **Weak guarantee** (30-day or less)
- [ ] ❌ **No urgency** (why buy now?)
- [ ] ❌ **No P.S.** (missing 30% of readers!)

## 🎯 THE MASTERS' WISDOM

**Eugene Schwartz:**
> "Copy cannot create desire. It can only intensify existing desire. Your only job is to channel that desire toward your product."

**Gary Halbert:**
> "The most important thing is the LIST (the starving crowd). Second is the OFFER. Third is the COPY. Headlines do 80% of the selling. Write 10+, test, pick the winner."

**Key Metrics (Target):**
- Conversion Rate: **2-5%** (cold traffic)
- Read Rate: **40%+** (full letter)
- P.S. Read Rate: **70%+** (many skip to this)
- Time on Page: **5+ minutes**
