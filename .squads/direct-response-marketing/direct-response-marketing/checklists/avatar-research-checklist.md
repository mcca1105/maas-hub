# Checklist: Avatar Research Quality

**Use when:** Completing the avatar research workflow or reviewing an existing avatar document.

---

## Section 1: Research Depth

- [ ] Research sourced from at least 3 distinct source categories (Amazon/Reddit/forums/YouTube/calls)
- [ ] Minimum 50 verbatim phrases collected before filtering to Top 20
- [ ] Verbatim quotes are in the avatar's exact words (not paraphrased or summarized)
- [ ] At least one source represents people who tried and FAILED with other solutions
- [ ] At least one source represents current customers / happy clients

## Section 2: Avatar Specificity

- [ ] Avatar is ONE named, specific person (not "women aged 25-45")
- [ ] Avatar has a specific job title or life situation
- [ ] Avatar's daily frustration is described in one specific sentence
- [ ] Avatar's "day before" and "day after" transformation are described
- [ ] Avatar's information sources are identified (where do they go for answers?)

## Section 3: Psychological Profile

- [ ] Dominant desire (surface level) is identified
- [ ] Core desire (deep level) is identified (security / love / status / freedom / health / meaning)
- [ ] Biggest fear is stated in their language (not ours)
- [ ] Previous solutions they've tried are listed
- [ ] Reason previous solutions failed (in their words) is documented

## Section 4: Language Profile

- [ ] Top 20 golden phrases are documented in verbatim form
- [ ] Metaphors they naturally use are identified
- [ ] Vocabulary level appropriate for this audience (technical / simple / emotional)
- [ ] Words they use for their problem (their label, not our label)
- [ ] Words they use for the solution they want

## Section 5: Market Awareness

- [ ] Eugene Schwartz awareness stage (1-5) is assigned with evidence
- [ ] Stage assignment has at least 3 supporting evidence points
- [ ] Counter-evidence (signs they might be in adjacent stage) is noted
- [ ] Copy strategy implications of the stage are documented

## Section 6: 5-Question Specificity Validation

- [ ] Can you picture a specific person? (YES required)
- [ ] Can you describe their specific Tuesday-morning frustration in 1 sentence? (YES)
- [ ] Do you know where they go for information? (YES)
- [ ] Can you name the last solution they tried that didn't work? (YES)
- [ ] Can you predict their first objection to your offer? (YES)

If fewer than 4 out of 5 = YES → Avatar is not specific enough. Return to research.

## Veto Conditions (any single VETO = reject and redo)

- VETO if avatar is described as a demographic range
- VETO if golden phrases are paraphrased
- VETO if awareness stage has no supporting evidence
- VETO if previous solutions tried is left blank
- VETO if avatar language matches marketing speak, not buyer speak
