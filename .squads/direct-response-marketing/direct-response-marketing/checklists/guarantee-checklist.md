# Checklist: Guarantee Quality

**Use when:** Reviewing a guarantee before embedding in copy.

---

## Section 1: Guarantee Fundamentals

- [ ] The guarantee has a specific, memorable name (not "Money-Back Guarantee")
- [ ] The guarantee terms are specific: exactly what happens, exactly how, exactly when
- [ ] The guarantee duration is stated clearly (30 days / 60 days / 1 year)
- [ ] The claiming process is simple and described (email / call / form — one step)
- [ ] The guarantee fully reverses risk for the buyer (they lose nothing if it fails)

## Section 2: Guarantee Type Fit

- [ ] Guarantee type matches the product and audience:
  - Satisfaction guarantee: Services, info products, subjective value
  - Results guarantee: Products with measurable, predictable results
  - Double-your-money: High-confidence offers with specific tracking
  - Keep-it guarantee: Low-cost products, lead magnets
  - Pay-only-if-results: Highest-risk tolerance, very clear metrics

## Section 3: Guarantee Copy

- [ ] Guarantee is positioned at two points in copy (first mention + near final CTA)
- [ ] Guarantee section opens by acknowledging the risk the buyer feels
- [ ] Guarantee language is bold and specific (not hedged or vague)
- [ ] Guarantee does not have hidden conditions ("as long as you complete every module")
- [ ] Guarantee section reframes: after the guarantee, the real risk is NOT buying

## Section 4: Business Considerations

- [ ] Guarantee is financially sustainable (refund rate won't destroy economics)
- [ ] Guarantee terms can actually be honored operationally
- [ ] Guarantee duration is appropriate for results timeframe (if promising 90-day results, guarantee should be 90+ days)
- [ ] Guarantee isn't so risk-free that it invites abuse (balance required)

## Section 5: Compliance

- [ ] Guarantee language complies with FTC requirements
- [ ] No claims in guarantee section that aren't substantiated elsewhere in copy
- [ ] Digital product guarantee complies with refund policy in terms of service

## Veto Conditions

- VETO if guarantee name is generic ("Satisfaction Guarantee" with no brand)
- VETO if claiming process requires multiple steps or contacting multiple people
- VETO if guarantee has hidden conditions ("provided you completed all steps")
- VETO if guarantee is not positioned near the CTA (must appear at point of purchase)
- VETO if guarantee duration is shorter than the time needed to see results
