# Checklist: Cialdini 7 Principles Audit

**Use when:** Auditing any sales page, email sequence, or funnel for persuasion completeness.

---

## Principle 1: Reciprocity

*Give before you ask. People feel obligated to return favors.*

- [ ] Lead magnet, free content, or valuable training delivered before the pitch
- [ ] Value given is genuinely useful (not just a teaser for the paid product)
- [ ] The gift is relevant to the offer (same audience, same problem domain)
- [ ] Reciprocity mechanism is explicit: "I want to give you X before anything else..."
- [ ] Free gift is NOT contingent on the sale

## Principle 2: Commitment & Consistency

*Small yeses lead to big yeses. People want to be consistent with past actions.*

- [ ] At least one micro-commitment is asked before the main offer (quiz / assessment / small optin)
- [ ] Copy references what they've already done ("Since you've already [action]...")
- [ ] Order form reminds them of their previous commitments
- [ ] Copy builds identity alignment ("As someone who [identity statement], you already know...")

**Where it appears in this copy:** ___________________

## Principle 3: Social Proof

*People follow what others like them are doing.*

- [ ] Specific testimonials with full name + location + specific result
- [ ] Numbers used where compelling (customers served, results achieved)
- [ ] Testimonials represent multiple sub-avatars (not all the same type of person)
- [ ] Social proof is positioned at the specific objection it addresses
- [ ] Video testimonials used where available

**Where it appears in this copy:** ___________________

## Principle 4: Authority

*People follow credible experts.*

- [ ] Credibility is established through story or results, not just title
- [ ] Relevant credentials are stated (credentials relevant to THIS problem, not general fame)
- [ ] Expert endorsements include the expert's credentials
- [ ] Published work, media mentions, or institutional affiliations cited if available
- [ ] Track record is specific (not "I've helped thousands" — "I've helped 4,231 people")

**Where it appears in this copy:** ___________________

## Principle 5: Liking

*People buy from those they know, like, and trust.*

- [ ] Personal story shared that makes the seller relatable and human
- [ ] Shared values or worldview explicitly stated
- [ ] Vulnerability or failure story included (not just success story)
- [ ] Similarities between seller and buyer highlighted
- [ ] Tone is warm and conversational (not corporate or formal)

**Where it appears in this copy:** ___________________

## Principle 6: Scarcity

*People want more of what they can have less of.*

- [ ] Scarcity mechanism is GENUINE (actually limited — will actually be enforced)
- [ ] Reason for scarcity is stated (why is it limited?)
- [ ] Deadline is specific (exact date, time, and timezone — not "soon" or "limited time")
- [ ] What specifically expires is clear (price / bonus / enrollment / product)
- [ ] Mechanism will actually be enforced (not a fake countdown timer)

**Where it appears in this copy:** ___________________

## Principle 7: Unity (from Pre-Suasion)

*We say yes to those who are in our group.*

- [ ] Shared identity established ("We are the kind of people who...")
- [ ] Shared enemy or shared challenge identified
- [ ] In-group language used (their vocabulary, their references)
- [ ] Community or tribe aspect of the offer highlighted
- [ ] Buyer is invited into an identity, not just offered a product

**Where it appears in this copy:** ___________________

## Overall Audit

- [ ] All 7 principles are represented somewhere in the funnel
- [ ] No single principle is over-relied upon (balanced use)
- [ ] Principles feel authentic (not checklist compliance)

## Veto Conditions

- VETO if scarcity mechanism is fake (no enforcement plan)
- VETO if social proof uses initials or anonymous sources
- VETO if authority is established only through title (no story or results)
- VETO if any 2 or more principles are completely absent from the funnel
