# Checklist: Headline Quality & Testing

**Use when:** Reviewing headlines before selecting finalists for testing.

---

## Section 1: Individual Headline Quality

For EACH headline under review:

- [ ] Headline is specific (contains number, name, timeframe, or mechanism — not vague)
- [ ] Headline speaks to a desire or fear the avatar has confirmed in VOC research
- [ ] Headline makes a claim the copy can substantiate (not over-promise)
- [ ] Headline is in active voice ("How to X" not "How X Can Be Achieved")
- [ ] Headline doesn't use "the best" or "the ultimate" without proof

## Section 2: Formula Coverage

The headline set should include at least one from each category:

- [ ] How-to headline: "How to [Specific Result] Without [Sacrifice]"
- [ ] Specific benefit headline: "[Result] in [Timeframe] — Guaranteed"
- [ ] Question headline: "Are You [Problem Situation]?"
- [ ] Social proof headline: "[Number] [Avatars] Have [Result] With [Method]"
- [ ] Mechanism headline: "The [Name] Method That [Claim]"
- [ ] Contrarian/pattern interrupt headline: "Stop [Common Recommendation]. Here's Why..."
- [ ] Warning headline: "Warning: [Common Action] Is [Unexpected Problem]"
- [ ] Story hook headline: "[Relatable Person] Was [Problem] Until [Discovery]"

## Section 3: Schwartz Awareness-Stage Fit

Headline matches the market's awareness stage:

- [ ] Stage 1 (Unaware): Headline leads with problem, not solution or product
- [ ] Stage 2 (Problem Aware): Headline leads with problem + hint of solution
- [ ] Stage 3 (Solution Aware): Headline leads with mechanism ("A New Way To...")
- [ ] Stage 4 (Product Aware): Headline leads with product advantage over alternatives
- [ ] Stage 5 (Most Aware): Headline leads with offer/price/promotion

## Section 4: Subheadline Review

- [ ] Subheadline expands the main headline (doesn't just repeat it)
- [ ] Subheadline adds specificity or proof
- [ ] Subheadline removes a potential objection to reading the copy
- [ ] Reading headline + subheadline together creates stronger desire than headline alone

## Section 5: Selection & Testing

- [ ] At least 5 headline options written before selecting finalists
- [ ] Finalists cover at least 3 different formula types
- [ ] A/B test structure defined (which 2 go head-to-head first)
- [ ] Secondary test queue defined (which runs next if both underperform)

## Veto Conditions

- VETO if headline makes a claim the copy cannot support
- VETO if headline requires reading the subheadline to make sense
- VETO if all headline options use the same formula type
- VETO if headline is over 12 words for above-fold placement
- VETO if headline contains "THE ULTIMATE" or "THE BEST" without proof
