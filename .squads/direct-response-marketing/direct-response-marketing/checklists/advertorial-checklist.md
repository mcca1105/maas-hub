# Checklist: Advertorial Quality

**Use when:** Reviewing an advertorial before publishing as native ad destination.

---

## Section 1: Editorial Integrity

- [ ] Headline reads like editorial journalism, not like an ad
- [ ] First paragraph establishes editorial credibility (discovery angle, report angle, story angle)
- [ ] Educational-to-commercial ratio is at least 70/30 (more teaching than selling)
- [ ] Article reads as independent content, not as a product brochure
- [ ] Product reveal is positioned as a "by the way" discovery, not the central pitch

## Section 2: Credibility Signals

- [ ] Source authority established in headline or byline (doctor, researcher, journalist, expert)
- [ ] At least one third-party reference (study, expert quote, published data)
- [ ] Claims are measured and journalistic (not hyperbolic)
- [ ] No superlatives in the first half ("best," "most powerful," "revolutionary")
- [ ] Author bio (if included) is specific and credible

## Section 3: Message Match with Native Ad

- [ ] Advertorial headline closely mirrors or extends the native ad headline
- [ ] First paragraph delivers on the curiosity created by the native ad
- [ ] Tone is consistent with the native ad hook
- [ ] Traffic temperature handled correctly (cold traffic needs more trust-building before product reveal)
- [ ] Advertorial doesn't feel like a bait-and-switch from the native ad

## Section 4: Educational Content Quality

- [ ] Educational section genuinely teaches something valuable
- [ ] Content would be interesting even without the product reveal
- [ ] The mechanism is explained fully before the product is introduced
- [ ] The reader understands WHY they need this solution before they're offered it
- [ ] At least one data point, case study, or expert quote supports the core claim

## Section 5: Product Transition

- [ ] Product is introduced naturally (as a conclusion to the educational content)
- [ ] Product reveal doesn't feel jarring or tonally different from the article
- [ ] Product name is introduced after the mechanism is established
- [ ] CTA is editorial-style ("Find out more" / "See the research") not ad-style ("Buy now")
- [ ] Disclosure label present (required: "Sponsored Content" or "Advertisement")

## Section 6: Technical

- [ ] Advertorial length: 800-1,200 words (health/finance), 500-800 words (lifestyle)
- [ ] Mobile-optimized formatting (short paragraphs, subheads)
- [ ] Images are editorial-looking (not stock-ad-looking product photos)
- [ ] Images have captions that add information
- [ ] Page load speed under 3 seconds

## Veto Conditions

- VETO if advertorial reads like a sales page (not editorial content)
- VETO if native ad headline promise isn't delivered in the first paragraph
- VETO if product is introduced in the first 30% of the article
- VETO if no disclosure label (legal requirement in most jurisdictions)
- VETO if educational content is under 40% of total word count
