---

## When to Use This SOP

- Creating a new VSL for any product or offer
- Rewriting a VSL that is not converting
- Building a VSL for a new market or audience segment

## Phase 1: Research (3-5 hours)

### 1.1 Prospect Research
- [ ] Define avatar: name, age, situation, daily frustrations
- [ ] Conduct VOC research (Amazon reviews, Reddit, forums, surveys)
- [ ] Extract exact prospect language (copy verbatim phrases)
- [ ] Identify top 3 pains, top 3 desires
- [ ] List everything they've already tried
- [ ] Document WHY each previous attempt failed (in their words)
- [ ] Identify their dream outcome (specific, vivid)

**VOC Research Sources:**
```
Health: Amazon reviews for supplements, Reddit (r/loseit, r/diabetes, etc.)
Finance: Reddit (r/personalfinance, r/investing), Quora
Relationships: Reddit, relationship books reviews
Business: LinkedIn, Reddit, Facebook groups
```

### 1.2 Market Research
- [ ] List top 3-5 competing products/VSLs
- [ ] Document their mechanisms and claims
- [ ] Identify market sophistication level (Schwartz 1-5)
- [ ] Find the white space (what no one else is saying)

### 1.3 Product Research
- [ ] Document every ingredient/component with its function
- [ ] Find supporting research (clinical studies, expert quotes)
- [ ] Map product benefits to specific prospect pains
- [ ] Identify the strongest claim you can substantiate

**Research Document Output:**
```markdown
PROSPECT AVATAR
```

## Phase 2: Mechanism (1-2 hours)

### 2.1 Identify the Mechanism

Answer: **Why does this product work when everything else the prospect tried has failed?**

Not the benefit ("you'll lose weight"). The mechanism ("The Cortisol Flush Protocol targets the specific hormone signaling pathway that causes visceral fat storage, which is why calorie restriction alone never worked for you.")

### 2.2 Mechanism Development Process

**Step 1:** List all the ways the product actually works (ingredients, compounds, processes)

**Step 2:** Find the one that is:
- Most believable (has real science or logic behind it)
- Most specific (not generic)
- Most new (prospect hasn't heard it before)
- Most relevant (directly solves their specific pain)

**Step 3:** Find or develop the "villain" — the hidden root cause the prospect hasn't addressed:
```
The real reason [problem persists] is not [what they think].
It's actually [hidden root cause].
That's why [common solutions fail].
[Mechanism Name] is the first [product category] that addresses [root cause].
```

**Step 4:** Name the mechanism:
- "[Adjective] [Noun] [System/Protocol/Formula/Method]"
- Examples: "Cellular Regeneration Protocol", "Metabolic Reset System"

### 2.3 Mechanism Validation
- [ ] Is it specific enough to be unique?
- [ ] Is it believable (science, logic, or proof behind it)?
- [ ] Is it new to the prospect?
- [ ] Does it directly address why their previous attempts failed?
- [ ] Can it be explained in under 60 seconds?

## Phase 4: VSL Script Structure

Write in this exact order:

### Section 1: HOOK (Minutes 0:00-2:00)

**Goal:** Stop the scroll. Earn the next 10 minutes of attention.

**Elements:**
- Pattern interrupt (shocking statement, counterintuitive claim, or unusual scenario)
- Audience qualification ("If you're [specific descriptor], this is for you")
- Immediate big promise (what they'll discover)
- Credibility signal (who this is from)

**Template (cold traffic, Stage 2 awareness):**
```
If you've been struggling with [problem] no matter how hard you try,
then pay close attention to this short video.

Because in the next [X] minutes, I'm going to show you [specific outcome]
— without [common sacrifice/obstacle].

My name is [Name], and what I'm about to share with you
changed [my life / the lives of thousands of people] who were in
the exact same position you are right now.
```

### Section 2: PROBLEM AGITATION (Minutes 2:00-5:00)

**Goal:** Make the prospect feel completely understood. Use their words.

**Elements:**
- Name the specific frustration (from VOC research, their language)
- Expand on the consequences (what staying stuck costs them)
- Identify the hidden cause (set up for mechanism reveal)
- Validation ("It's not your fault. Here's why.")

**Rule:** This section should make the prospect think "How do they know exactly what I'm going through?"

### Section 3: STORY / CREDIBILITY (Minutes 5:00-10:00)

**Goal:** Establish trust. Prove you understand and have a solution.

**Elements:**
- The protagonist's story (founder, patient, research subject)
- Their specific "before" situation (mirror the prospect)
- The discovery of the mechanism (the turning point)
- The transformation (specific, vivid, measured)

### Section 4: MECHANISM REVEAL (Minutes 10:00-16:00)

**Goal:** Show them exactly why this works and why previous solutions failed.

**Elements:**
- The villain (the real root cause they haven't addressed)
- The mechanism name (introduced with gravitas)
- How it works (simple, visual explanation)
- Scientific/logical proof
- Why previous solutions don't address this root cause
- Why YOUR solution is the first to address it

### Section 5: PRODUCT REVEAL (Minutes 16:00-20:00)

**Goal:** Introduce the product as the vessel of the mechanism.

**Elements:**
- Product name and what it is
- Each component and its mechanism-specific function
- How to use it
- Timeline for results (realistic, specific)

### Section 6: PROOF STACK (Minutes 20:00-25:00)

**Goal:** Make the result irrefutable.

**Elements:**
- 5-10 testimonials (specific results, real names, photos if possible)
- Clinical data if available
- Before/after documentation
- Expert endorsements if relevant

**Testimony format:**
```
"[Specific result with numbers] in [timeframe]."
— [Full Name], [City, State], [Occupation if relevant]
```

### Section 7: OFFER REVEAL (Minutes 25:00-30:00)

**Goal:** Present an irresistible deal.

**Order:**
1. Transition: "Now, here's what I want you to do"
2. Product overview (re-state what they're getting)
3. Bonus reveal (each with name, what it does, standalone value)
4. Total value stack (add up all values)
5. Price anchor (mention higher prices, what others pay)
6. Your price reveal
7. Guarantee (state terms clearly, transfer all risk)
8. Urgency/scarcity (real reason, specific)

### Section 8: CALL TO ACTION (Minutes 30:00-32:00)

**Goal:** Remove final friction and get the click.

**Elements:**
- Clear instruction: "Click the button below now"
- Reiterate guarantee ("You risk nothing")
- Final urgency reminder
- First CTA

### Section 9: P.S. SUMMARY (Text on page, below video)

- Biggest benefit restated
- Guarantee restated
- Price + urgency restated
- Second CTA

## Quality Review Checklist

Before submitting for production:

**Structure:**
- [ ] Hook pattern-interrupts in first 30 seconds
- [ ] Prospect problem named in their exact language
- [ ] Mechanism has a name and is explained clearly
- [ ] Proof stack has specific results (not vague praise)
- [ ] Offer includes price, bonuses, guarantee, urgency
- [ ] CTA is specific and repeated

**Copy Quality:**
- [ ] Written at 6th-8th grade reading level
- [ ] Short sentences (average under 15 words)
- [ ] Active voice throughout
- [ ] "You" count exceeds "we/I" count
- [ ] No weasel words ("might", "could", "try", "maybe")
- [ ] Every claim is specific (numbers, names, studies)

**Awareness Match:**
- [ ] Awareness stage identified
- [ ] Opening matches that stage (Schwartz framework)
- [ ] No assumptions of knowledge the prospect doesn't have
