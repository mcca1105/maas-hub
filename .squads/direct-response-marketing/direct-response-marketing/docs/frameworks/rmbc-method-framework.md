# Stefan Georgi — RMBC Method Framework

**Source:** The RMBC Method by Stefan Georgi (2014, RMBC II 2023)
**Squad Agent:** `@drm:stefan-georgi`
**Application:** VSL creation, sales letter writing, mechanism identification

---

## Overview

RMBC stands for **Research → Mechanism → Brief → Copy**.

Stefan Georgi developed RMBC in 2014 to write 12 sales letters per month at a 90%+ control rate in the health and financial niches. The method's core insight: **most copywriters start writing too early**. RMBC is a pre-writing system that ensures when you finally write, you already know exactly what to say.

> "By the time you start writing the actual copy, 80% of the work is already done."
> — Stefan Georgi

## Phase 1: Research

**Objective:** Understand the prospect so deeply that you can write copy in their voice, not yours.

### What to Research

**1. The Prospect**
- Demographics: age, gender, income, occupation
- Psychographics: worldview, fears, desires, identity
- Awareness stage (per Schwartz): What do they already know?
- Traffic temperature: Cold / Warm / Hot

**2. Voice of Customer (VOC)**
- Amazon reviews for competitive products (1-star AND 5-star)
- Reddit threads in relevant subreddits
- Forum discussions (Quora, niche forums)
- YouTube comment sections
- Direct customer interviews / surveys
- Testimonials on competitor pages

**3. The Market**
- What products already exist?
- What claims have already been made?
- What mechanisms have been used?
- What is the market's sophistication level? (Schwartz framework)

**4. The Product**
- Unique ingredients / components
- The mechanism of action (how does it actually work?)
- Scientific backing (studies, clinical data)
- Origin story of the product
- Who created it and why

**5. The Competition**
- Top 3-5 competitor offers
- Their headlines and hooks
- Their mechanisms and claims
- Their price points and guarantees
- What they're doing wrong (positioning opportunity)

### Research Tools
- Google, Google Scholar
- Amazon book reviews
- Reddit, Quora
- PubMed (for health)
- JSTOR (for academic)
- Competitor landing pages / VSLs
- Customer surveys / interviews

### Research Output
Fill out the Research Document:
```
- Who is the prospect in their own words?
- What are their 3 biggest pains?
- What are their 3 biggest desires?
- What language do they use to describe their problem?
- What have they tried that didn't work?
- Why did it fail (in their mind)?
- What is their dream outcome?
- What objections will they have?
```

## Phase 2: Mechanism

**Objective:** Identify or engineer the unique mechanism that makes your product categorically different from everything else the prospect has tried.

### What is a Mechanism?

A mechanism is **the specific reason why your solution works when others don't**. It's not a benefit ("lose weight"). It's the *why behind the benefit* ("The Cortisol Flush Protocol targets the specific hormone that causes belly fat storage, which is why calorie-cutting alone never worked for you").

### Mechanism Formula

```
[Prospect's core problem] happens because of [Hidden Root Cause].
Traditional solutions fail because they don't address [Root Cause].
[Product] uses [Unique Mechanism Name] to [How It Fixes Root Cause],
which is why it produces [Benefit] even when nothing else has worked.
```

### Types of Mechanisms

**1. Ingredient/Component Mechanism**
A specific compound, ingredient, or formula element that produces the result.
*Example: "The Japanese Sencha Extract in [Product] activates AMPK pathways that..."*

**2. Process Mechanism**
A proprietary sequence or protocol that creates the result.
*Example: "The 3-Phase Reset Protocol retrains the body's stress response by..."*

**3. Technology Mechanism**
A specific technology or delivery system that makes the product work differently.
*Example: "Our Nano-Emulsion delivery system ensures 10x higher absorption than..."*

**4. Discovery Mechanism**
A hidden truth, research finding, or insight that reframes the problem.
*Example: "A 2019 Harvard study revealed that the real cause of [problem] isn't [common belief] but..."*

### Mechanism Quality Test
A good mechanism must pass all 3:
- [ ] Believable (has logical/scientific basis)
- [ ] Specific (not vague — names the exact thing)
- [ ] New (prospect hasn't heard this before)

### Naming the Mechanism
Give the mechanism a proprietary name. This creates differentiation even if the underlying science is similar to competitors.
- Format: "[Adjective] [Noun] [Process/System/Protocol/Method/Formula]"
- Examples: "Cellular Reset Protocol", "Metabolic Ignition System", "Cortisol Flush Formula"

## Phase 3: Brief

**Objective:** Answer all key copy decisions BEFORE writing. The Brief is the blueprint. The copy is just construction.

### The Brief Document

Answer every section before touching the copy:

```
PROSPECT
- Who specifically am I writing to?
- What stage of awareness are they at?
- What's their dominant emotion right now?

OFFER
- What is the core product?
- What is the price?
- What bonuses are included?
- What is the guarantee?
- What is the urgency/scarcity element?

MECHANISM
- What is the mechanism name?
- In one sentence: why does it work?
- What proof supports the mechanism?

HEADLINE OPTIONS
- Write 5-10 potential headlines
- Star the top 3 to test

STORY / HOOK
- What is the opening story or hook?
- Whose story? (customer avatar? founder? research discovery?)
- What's the emotional arc? (Problem → struggle → discovery → transformation)

PROOF STACK
- How many testimonials? From whom?
- Any clinical studies?
- Before/after data?
- Celebrity or expert endorsement?

OBJECTIONS
- List every objection the prospect might have
- What's the answer to each?

CALL TO ACTION
- What exactly should they do?
- What happens after they click/call/order?
```

### Brief Quality Check
- [ ] I know exactly who I'm writing to
- [ ] The mechanism is named and explained
- [ ] I have at least 10 headline options
- [ ] I know the complete offer (price, bonuses, guarantee)
- [ ] I've identified every major objection
- [ ] I know what proof I have available

## Phase 4: Copy

**Objective:** Write the copy. With R, M, and B complete, this is mechanical, not creative.

### VSL / Sales Letter Structure (RMBC Standard)

```
1. HOOK (0-3 min for VSL / first 200 words for sales letter)
   - Pattern interrupt
   - Big promise or shocking claim
   - Who this is for

2. STORY / CREDIBILITY
   - Protagonist the prospect identifies with
   - The problem (in their language from research)
   - The failure (they tried everything and it didn't work)
   - The discovery (how they found the mechanism)

3. MECHANISM EXPLANATION
   - Name the mechanism
   - Explain why it works (simply, specifically)
   - Prove it (studies, logic, visual demonstration)
   - Show why previous solutions didn't have this

4. PRODUCT REVEAL
   - Introduce the product as the vessel of the mechanism
   - What's inside / how it works in practice

5. PROOF STACK
   - Testimonials (specific results, not vague praise)
   - Clinical data if applicable
   - Before/after documentation

6. OFFER
   - What they get
   - The price (revealed after value is established)
   - Bonuses (each with standalone value)
   - Guarantee (eliminate all risk)

7. URGENCY / SCARCITY
   - Real reason for limited time or quantity
   - Consequence of not acting

8. CALL TO ACTION
   - Clear, specific instruction
   - Repeat 2-3 times
   - Overcome final hesitation

9. P.S.
   - Re-state the biggest benefit
   - Re-state the guarantee
   - Re-state the urgency
```

### Copy Quality Standards
- Written at 6th-8th grade reading level
- No passive voice (active throughout)
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs.
- Every paragraph creates a reason to read the next
- Specificity over generality at all times
