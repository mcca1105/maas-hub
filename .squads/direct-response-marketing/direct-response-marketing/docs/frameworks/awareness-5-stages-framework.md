# Eugene Schwartz — 5 Stages of Market Awareness Framework

**Source:** Breakthrough Advertising (1966) by Eugene M. Schwartz
**Squad Agent:** `@drm:eugene-schwartz`
**Application:** Determines headline strategy, copy depth, and selling angle for any market

---

## The Core Insight

> "The more aware your prospect is, the less selling you have to do."
> — Eugene Schwartz

Awareness is not about whether someone knows your product. It's about **how much they know about their own problem and the solutions that exist**. Copy that doesn't match the prospect's awareness stage will fail — no matter how well-written it is.

The copy must meet the prospect where they are, not where you wish they were.

---

## The 5 Stages

### Stage 1 — Unaware

**Who they are:** The prospect does not know they have a problem. They may be experiencing symptoms but haven't connected them to a root cause. They have no vocabulary for their issue.

**What they're thinking:** Nothing specific. They're living life.

**Copy strategy:**
- Start with a pattern interrupt — a shocking statement, a counterintuitive claim, a question that forces self-recognition
- Lead with the hidden problem, the invisible consequence, or the surprising truth
- Never mention your product in the headline
- Use "story" or "news" format to reveal information they didn't know

**Headline approach:**
- "The Silent Killer Hidden in Your [Daily Habit]"
- "Warning: What Most [Target] Don't Know About [Problem]"
- "Scientists Discover: [Surprising Revelation About Their Life]"

**Copy length:** Long. You must first make them aware of the problem before you can offer a solution.

### Stage 2 — Problem Aware

**Who they are:** The prospect knows they have a problem. They feel frustrated. They've Googled symptoms. They've complained to friends. They haven't found a satisfying solution yet.

**What they're thinking:** "Why can't I fix this? I've tried everything. There must be something I'm missing."

**Copy strategy:**
- Open by naming their frustration precisely (use their words from VOC research)
- Validate that the problem is real, not their fault
- Position your solution as a new approach they haven't tried
- Emphasize that the solutions they've tried failed because they were incomplete, not because they couldn't succeed

**Headline approach:**
- "If You're [Tired/Frustrated/Stuck] With [Problem], Read This"
- "Why Nothing You've Tried For [Problem] Has Worked — And What Does"
- "The Real Reason You Can't [Desired Outcome] No Matter How Hard You Try"

**Copy length:** Long-to-medium. Validate the problem extensively, then pivot to solution.

**Primary agent:** `@drm:gary-halbert`
**Secondary agent:** `@drm:eugene-schwartz`

---

### Stage 3 — Solution Aware

**Who they are:** The prospect knows solutions exist. They may have tried supplements, programs, therapy, coaching, or other products. They're comparison shopping but haven't found the right fit.

**What they're thinking:** "I know there are solutions. Why hasn't any of them worked for me specifically?"

**Copy strategy:**
- Introduce a **unique mechanism** — why existing solutions fail and why yours is categorically different
- Don't talk about the problem (they know it). Talk about the mechanism that fixes it
- Use "unlike other methods" language
- Position as "the new approach that finally works"

**Headline approach:**
- "A New [Method/System/Discovery] That [Benefit] Without [Common Objection]"
- "Finally: The Real Reason [Other Solutions] Failed — And What Actually Works"
- "Breakthrough [Category]: How [Unique Mechanism] Delivers [Desired Outcome]"

**Copy length:** Medium. Skip problem setup, go to mechanism quickly.

**Primary agent:** `@drm:stefan-georgi` (VSL + mechanism)
**Secondary agent:** `@drm:eugene-schwartz`

---

### Stage 4 — Product Aware

**Who they are:** The prospect knows your product. They've seen your ads, read your landing page, maybe watched your VSL. They haven't bought. They have objections.

**What they're thinking:** "Is this the right product for me? Is it worth the price? Can I trust this company?"

**Copy strategy:**
- Overcome specific objections directly
- Reinforce proof: testimonials, case studies, clinical data, before/after
- Sharpen the offer with bonuses, guarantees, payment plans
- Use comparison to other products (frame yours as superior)

**Headline approach:**
- "Here's Exactly What Happens When You [Use Product]"
- "Why [X] People Have Already [Result] With [Product]"
- "What Our [Customers/Members] Are Saying About [Product]"

**Copy length:** Short-to-medium. They know the context. Go straight to reassurance and offer.

**Primary agent:** `@drm:robert-cialdini` (proof + persuasion)
**Secondary agent:** `@drm:gary-halbert`

---

### Stage 5 — Most Aware

**Who they are:** The prospect knows your product, knows the price, knows what it does, and is ready to buy. They just haven't pulled the trigger yet.

**What they're thinking:** "Tell me the deal. What's the price, what do I get, how do I order?"

**Copy strategy:**
- Lead directly with the offer
- State name, price, and deal in the headline
- Emphasize urgency and scarcity (real ones only)
- Remove friction from the buying process
- Pile on bonuses

**Headline approach:**
- "[Product Name] — Now [X]% Off"
- "Last Chance: [Product] at [Price] Ends [Deadline]"
- "Order [Product] Today and Get [Bonus] Free"

**Copy length:** Short. They know everything. Just make it easy to say yes.

**Secondary agent:** `@drm:ryan-deiss` (offer structure)

## Market Sophistication — The Second Dimension

Beyond awareness, Schwartz identified **market sophistication**: how many similar products and claims a market has already been exposed to.

| Sophistication Level | Market State | Copy Strategy |
|---------------------|-------------|---------------|
| Level 1 | First product of its kind | State the claim directly |
| Level 2 | Competitors exist | Amplify the claim (bigger, faster, stronger) |
| Level 3 | All claims are amplified | Introduce a new mechanism |
| Level 4 | Mechanisms are commoditized | Associate mechanism with an identity/tribe |
| Level 5 | Full saturation | Pioneer a new market segment entirely |

**Rule:** As sophistication increases, you can never go backward. You must always match or exceed what the market has already heard.

---

## Mass Desire — The Foundation

Schwartz's ultimate insight: **you cannot create desire. You can only channel existing desire.**

Every successful ad taps into a mass desire that already exists in the market. Your job is to:
1. Identify the strongest, most urgent desire your market already has
2. Connect your product to that desire with a believable, specific mechanism
3. Intensify the desire through vivid language and proof

> "The mass desire must be already there — waiting to be tapped. Your job is not to create it but to channel it."

## DRM Application Protocol

When a copy request comes in, the DRM Chief runs this diagnostic:

```
1. What awareness stage is this prospect at?
   → If unknown → run *awareness diagnosis before any copy work

2. What's the traffic temperature?
   → Cold traffic = Stage 1 or 2 approach regardless of awareness
   → Warm traffic = match actual awareness stage
   → Hot traffic = Stage 4 or 5 approach

3. What's the market sophistication level?
   → Determines whether to use claim, mechanism, or identity play

4. Does copy match the diagnosis?
   → If no → route back to specialist to realign
```
