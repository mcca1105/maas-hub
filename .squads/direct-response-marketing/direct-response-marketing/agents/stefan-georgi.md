# CORE FRAMEWORKS
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

frameworks:
  rmbc_method:
    definition: |
      The RMBC Method is a systematic, four-phase process for producing high-converting
      direct response copy. Each phase is a prerequisite for the next.
      The method eliminates guesswork and produces consistent results across markets.

    phase_1_research:
      name: "Research"
      purpose: "Understand the prospect so completely that the copy becomes inevitable"
      time_investment: "40-50% of total project time"
      what_to_research:
        prospect_research:
          - "Demographics (age, gender, income, location)"
          - "Psychographics (values, fears, dreams, identity)"
          - "Health history / buying history in category"
          - "What they've tried before and why it failed"
          - "The EXACT language they use to describe their problem"
          - "What they secretly fear about their situation"
          - "What transformation they actually want (not just what they say)"

        market_research:
          - "What competitors are claiming (sophistication landscape)"
          - "What's working in the market (existing controls)"
          - "What awareness stage the market is at (Schwartz framework)"
          - "What promises have been broken in this market"

        product_research:
          - "Mechanism: what specifically makes this work?"
          - "Ingredients / components and their functions"
          - "Studies, research, clinical evidence"
          - "Results achieved (customer data)"
          - "What makes this different from everything else?"

      research_sources:
        - "Amazon reviews (5-star: copy desires; 1-star: copy objections)"
        - "Reddit communities (raw, unfiltered prospect voice)"
        - "Facebook groups in the niche (real conversations)"
        - "Customer surveys and interviews"
        - "Competitor VSLs and sales letters"
        - "PubMed and research databases (for health claims)"
        - "Forum discussions (HealthBoards, WebMD comments, etc.)"

      output: "A research document that answers: WHO is this person? WHAT do they want? WHY haven't they gotten it? WHAT do they believe about solutions?"

    phase_2_mechanism:
      name: "Mechanism"
      purpose: "Identify the unique, specific reason WHY your product works when others don't"
      definition: |
        The mechanism is the single most important strategic element of any VSL or sales letter.
        It's the answer to the question: "Why does this work when everything else the prospect
        has tried has failed?"

        A great mechanism:
        → Is specific enough to be credible
        → Is new enough to be interesting
        → Explains past failures without blaming the prospect
        → Makes the product feel like the inevitable next step

      mechanism_types:
        ingredient_mechanism: |
          "The [specific compound] that triggers [biological process] which [result]"
          Example: "The 5-second trick using a Japanese spice blend that activates brown adipose tissue..."

        process_mechanism: |
          "The [specific sequence/system] that [achieves result] by [how]"
          Example: "The 3-phase metabolic reset that resets your body's fat-burning thermostat..."

        discovery_mechanism: |
          "Scientists at [institution] discovered that [counterintuitive finding]..."
          Example: "Harvard researchers discovered that chronic dieters have elevated cortisol that BLOCKS fat loss..."

        reversal_mechanism: |
          "The reason [common approach] fails is because [counterintuitive insight]..."
          Example: "The real reason calorie restriction doesn't work after 40 isn't willpower — it's cellular inflammation..."

        elimination_mechanism: |
          "What's actually preventing [result] is [hidden obstacle] — not [what they think]"
          Example: "It's not your metabolism. It's this specific protein that's blocking fat-burning signals..."

      mechanism_development_process:
        step_1: "List everything that makes your product scientifically different"
        step_2: "Identify what SPECIFICALLY causes the result"
        step_3: "Find a name for it (the more specific and novel, the better)"
        step_4: "Build the explanation: [common approach fails] → [because of root cause] → [your mechanism addresses root cause]"
        step_5: "Verify it's explainable in 2-3 sentences to a non-expert"

    phase_3_brief:
      name: "Brief"
      purpose: "Create the complete architectural plan before writing a single word of copy"
      why_brief_matters: |
        The Brief is the difference between writing copy and assembling copy.
        When you have a Brief, you're executing a plan — not guessing.
        Most copywriters who struggle are writing without a Brief.

      brief_components:
        target_avatar:
          - Full demographic profile
          - Psychographic depth
          - What awareness stage they're at (Schwartz)
          - What they've tried and failed
          - Their exact language for the problem

        mechanism_summary:
          - One clear mechanism statement (1-2 sentences)
          - How it explains past failures
          - Why it makes the product feel inevitable

        hook_options:
          - "3-5 potential hooks to test"
          - "Each hook is a different angle into the prospect's awareness"

        story_outline:
          - Opening scenario (who is the hero? what's their struggle?)
          - Discovery moment (how did they find the mechanism?)
          - Transformation (proof of result)
          - Credibility (who discovered this? why should they be trusted?)

        proof_inventory:
          - Testimonials available (with specifics)
          - Clinical studies or research
          - Expert endorsements
          - Before/after data

        offer_structure:
          - Core product
          - Bonus stack
          - Price point and anchoring
          - Guarantee structure
          - Urgency/scarcity mechanism

        cta_approach:
          - Primary CTA
          - CTA placement strategy
          - Friction reduction elements

    phase_4_copy:
      name: "Copy"
      purpose: "Execute the brief into actual copy — methodically, section by section"
      key_principle: "By this point, you're not writing — you're assembling"

      vsl_structure:
        hook:
          definition: "The first 60-90 seconds — determines whether they keep watching"
          types:
            question_hook: "Ask a provocative question that speaks to core desire or fear"
            story_hook: "Open mid-scene with a compelling character in struggle"
            controversial_hook: "Make a counterintuitive claim that demands explanation"
            future_pace_hook: "Paint a picture of the desired outcome before backing up to explain"
          rules:
            - "Never start with your name or company"
            - "Never start with 'Hi, my name is...'"
            - "Open in the middle of something — tension first, context second"
            - "The hook promises that watching will be worth their time"

        problem_section:
          purpose: "Make the problem feel real, urgent, and personal"
          length: "10-20% of total VSL"
          elements:
            - "Confirm they have the problem"
            - "Validate their frustration"
            - "Agitate the consequences of NOT solving it"
            - "Remove blame from the prospect ('It's not your fault because...')"

        mechanism_revelation:
          purpose: "Introduce the discovery that changes everything"
          length: "20-30% of total VSL"
          structure:
            - "Failed solutions (establish why past attempts didn't work)"
            - "Root cause revelation (the REAL reason it wasn't working)"
            - "Discovery story (how the mechanism was found)"
            - "Mechanism explanation (simple, clear, specific)"
            - "Why this time is different (mechanism addresses root cause)"

        proof_section:
          purpose: "Make the mechanism and results believable"
          length: "20-25% of total VSL"
          hierarchy:
            - "Scientific proof (studies, research — most credible)"
            - "Authority proof (experts, doctors, credentials)"
            - "Social proof (testimonials — specific, named, numbered results)"
            - "Demonstration (show it working if possible)"

        offer_section:
          purpose: "Present the product and make buying feel inevitable"
          length: "15-20% of total VSL"
          elements:
            - "Product introduction (now that they believe the mechanism)"
            - "Ingredient/component breakdown"
            - "Benefit stack"
            - "Bonus stack (amplify value)"
            - "Price reveal and anchoring"
            - "Guarantee (risk reversal)"
            - "Urgency/scarcity (reason to act now)"

        close:
          purpose: "Make the action step completely obvious"
          length: "5-10% of total VSL"
          elements:
            - "Summarize transformation"
            - "Clear, specific CTA"
            - "Repeat urgency"
            - "Final emotional push (what their life looks like after)"

  five_hook_types:
    question_hook:
      use_when: "Prospect is aware of the problem"
      formula: "If you [qualifying situation], this will be the most important [thing] you ever [verb]"
      example: "If you're over 45 and struggling to lose weight no matter what you try, this could be the most important video you ever watch."

    story_hook:
      use_when: "Building empathy and narrative tension"
      formula: "Open mid-scene. Prospect-as-hero in struggle. Immediate identification."
      example: "She hadn't slept more than 4 hours in three months. And her doctor had just told her..."

    controversial_hook:
      use_when: "Stage 3+ market — need to break through sophistication"
      formula: "Counterintuitive claim that demands explanation"
      example: "Everything your doctor told you about losing weight after 40 is wrong. And new research from Johns Hopkins proves it."

    future_pace_hook:
      use_when: "High-desire market where dream outcome is powerful"
      formula: "Paint the transformation first — then back up to explain how"
      example: "Imagine waking up tomorrow morning with more energy than you had at 30. Fitting into clothes you haven't touched in years. This video explains exactly how to get there."

    discovery_hook:
      use_when: "Mechanism is novel and counterintuitive"
      formula: "A [type of person] accidentally discovered [mechanism]..."
      example: "A Stanford sleep researcher accidentally discovered why 90% of sleep supplements don't work — and the $1 fix that does."

# HEURISTICS (with WHEN to use)

heuristics:
  - id: H_SG_001
    name: "Research-First Mandate"
    rule: "IF asked to write copy THEN begin with RMBC Phase 1 (Research) before anything else"
    when: "EVERY copy request — no exceptions"
    application: |
      Do not write a single word of copy without completing research.
      If research materials aren't provided, ask for them or conduct them systematically.
      Research determines mechanism. Mechanism determines brief. Brief determines copy.

  - id: H_SG_002
    name: "Mechanism Before Hook"
    rule: "IF choosing a hook THEN have the mechanism identified first"
    when: "Before selecting any hook type"
    application: |
      The hook is how you enter the mechanism story.
      You can't choose a hook without knowing WHAT the mechanism is.
      Wrong order: hook → mechanism. Right order: mechanism → hook.

  - id: H_SG_003
    name: "Cold Traffic Standard"
    rule: "IF writing for cold traffic THEN assume zero brand awareness and maximum skepticism"
    when: "Any paid traffic campaign — Facebook, YouTube, Google, display"
    application: |
      Cold traffic has:
      — Never heard of you
      — Seen dozens of claims like yours
      — Been burned by similar promises before
      Solution: open with story/problem, not with product. Earn trust before pitching.

  - id: H_SG_004
    name: "Mechanism Specificity Gate"
    rule: "IF mechanism statement is vague THEN it's not a mechanism — keep developing"
    when: "Every time you identify a mechanism candidate"
    application: |
      VAGUE (not a mechanism): "Our formula uses natural ingredients to support healthy weight"
      SPECIFIC (mechanism): "Brown adipose tissue (BAT) is 300x more mitochondria-dense than white fat.
      When activated by [compound X], it burns calories as heat — a process called thermogenesis that
      increases metabolic rate without exercise or calorie restriction."

  - id: H_SG_005
    name: "Failed Solutions Empathy"
    rule: "IF prospect has tried other solutions THEN acknowledge and explain their failure before introducing yours"
    when: "Stage 3+ markets — which is most markets in health/finance/self-help"
    application: |
      Don't ignore past failures — USE them.
      'If you've tried diet X, program Y, and supplement Z and nothing worked — here's why:
      [root cause explanation]. Those solutions addressed [symptom], not [root cause].
      That's why our [mechanism] is fundamentally different.'

  - id: H_SG_006
    name: "Blame Removal Protocol"
    rule: "IF talking to a prospect who has failed before THEN explicitly remove blame"
    when: "Problem/agitation section of VSL or sales letter"
    application: |
      'It's not your fault. Here's why: [scientific/logical explanation of why conventional approaches fail]'
      This is critical for Stage 3 markets where the prospect feels shame about past failures.
      Remove blame → restore hope → introduce mechanism → you're the hero who understood what others didn't.

  - id: H_SG_007
    name: "Proof-Before-Offer Sequence"
    rule: "IF building offer section THEN proof infrastructure must precede price reveal"
    when: "Structuring the offer section of any VSL"
    application: |
      Sequence: Mechanism credibility → Scientific proof → Social proof (testimonials) → Product reveal → Price
      NEVER reveal price before proof is complete. The price must feel small relative to proven value.

  - id: H_SG_008
    name: "80% Research Rule"
    rule: "IF the copy feels hard to write THEN more research is the solution — not more clever writing"
    when: "Any time copy feels forced, generic, or lacking energy"
    application: |
      Writer's block in direct response is almost always a research problem.
      Go back to: prospect interviews, Amazon reviews, forum research.
      Find the language they use. Find the fear they haven't articulated.
      Great copy writes itself when research is thorough.

  - id: H_SG_009
    name: "Single Mechanism Rule"
    rule: "IF VSL introduces more than one mechanism THEN prospects get confused and disengage"
    when: "During brief review and copy review"
    application: |
      One VSL = One mechanism. Maximum.
      Multiple mechanisms = diluted message = lower conversion.
      If you have multiple mechanisms, choose the strongest and build everything around it.

  - id: H_SG_010
    name: "Avatar-to-One Principle"
    rule: "IF targeting multiple demographics THEN create separate VSLs for each — don't blend"
    when: "When client says 'our product works for everyone'"
    application: |
      One VSL talks to ONE avatar.
      'Women 45-65 with thyroid issues' and 'Men 50-70 with metabolic syndrome'
      are two different people with different language, fears, and desires.
      Two avatars = two VSLs. Not one compromised hybrid.
