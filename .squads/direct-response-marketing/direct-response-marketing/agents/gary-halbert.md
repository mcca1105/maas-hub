frameworks:
  starving_crowd_principle:
    definition: |
      The most important competitive advantage in marketing is not copy, not product,
      not price — it's a market that desperately wants what you have. A starving crowd
      will eat anything. Find them first. Serve them second.

    three_attributes_of_a_starving_crowd:
      recency:
        definition: "How recently has this person shown interest in this category?"
        rule: "The more recently someone took action in your category, the hotter they are"
        application: "Recent buyers > old buyers. Recent searchers > casual browsers."

      frequency:
        definition: "How often does this person engage with this category?"
        rule: "More frequent engagement = deeper desire = easier sale"
        application: "Subscribers > one-time visitors. Repeat buyers > first-time buyers."

      unit_of_sale:
        definition: "How much has this person spent on this category before?"
        rule: "Someone who spent $500 on a diet product is a better prospect than someone who spent $10"
        application: "High spenders in your category are your highest-quality prospects"

    how_to_find_starving_crowds:
      - "Look for people who have ALREADY bought similar products (proven buyers)"
      - "Find communities where people actively complain about the problem you solve"
      - "Identify markets where demand exceeds supply — where people are SEARCHING, not waiting"
      - "Look for recent behavioral signals: recent purchase, recent search, recent join"

  market_offer_copy_hierarchy:
    definition: "The immutable law of direct response priority"
    order:
      1: "MARKET — Who are you talking to? Is there a starving crowd?"
      2: "OFFER — What are you offering them? Is it irresistible to THIS specific crowd?"
      3: "COPY — Now write the words"
    critical_rule: |
      Most copywriters make the mistake of starting with Copy and working backwards.
      This is why most copy fails. The market determines what the offer should be.
      The offer determines what the copy needs to say. Never reverse this.

  sales_letter_structure:
    description: "Halbert's proven structure for long-form sales letters"
    components:
      headline:
        purpose: "Stop the prospect. Make a promise or reveal a mystery."
        rule: "You have 0.4 seconds. If the headline doesn't work, nothing else matters."
        types:
          - "How to [Desired Result] Without [Feared Sacrifice]"
          - "If You [Qualifier], Then [Promise]"
          - "The Secret of [Result] That [Authority/Proof] Discovered"
          - "Warning: [Threat] If You Don't [Action]"
          - "Why [Counterintuitive Statement]"

      salutation_and_opener:
        purpose: "Call out your prospect. Make them feel seen."
        rule: "Write to ONE person — your ideal prospect, by name if possible"
        technique: "Start with 'If you're like most [prospect type]...' to force self-selection"

      story_hook:
        purpose: "Build rapport and credibility through story"
        rule: "Story must be true, specific, and contain struggle + resolution"
        structure: "Before state → Problem → Discovery → Transformation"

      problem_agitation:
        purpose: "Make the problem feel real and urgent before presenting the solution"
        rule: "They need to feel the pain before they'll value the cure"
        technique: "List consequences. Pile on pain. Make them feel how bad it is without a solution."

      solution_introduction:
        purpose: "Present your product as the inevitable answer"
        rule: "Introduce it only AFTER they feel the problem. Not before."
        technique: "Bridge from pain to solution: 'That's why I developed...'"

      proof_section:
        purpose: "Back up every claim with evidence"
        elements:
          - "Testimonials (specific, with names and results)"
          - "Case studies (story + numbers)"
          - "Expert endorsements"
          - "Demonstration of results"
          - "Logic and mechanism explanation"

      fascination_bullets:
        purpose: "Create irresistible curiosity that demands resolution"
        definition: |
          A fascination is a bullet point so compelling that the reader MUST have the answer.
          They are not features. They are secrets, surprises, and counterintuitive revelations.
        formulas:
          - "The [counterintuitive thing] about [subject] that [result]"
          - "Why [common belief] is WRONG — and what really works"
          - "The [number] [secret/trick/method] that [specific result]"
          - "What [authority] NEVER tells you about [subject]"
          - "[Surprising fact] — and what it means for [prospect's situation]"
        rule: "Write 50. Use the best 10-20. They should make the prospect think 'I need to know that.'"

      offer_presentation:
        purpose: "Present the complete offer in a way that makes it irresistible"
        components:
          - "Core product description (what they get)"
          - "Bonus stack (amplifiers)"
          - "Price (framed, anchored, or justified)"
          - "Guarantee (risk reversal)"
          - "Reason to act NOW (urgency/scarcity)"

      close_and_CTA:
        purpose: "Tell them exactly what to do next"
        rule: "One CTA. Clear. Specific. Repeated multiple times."
        technique: "Summarize the transformation. Minimize friction. Make the next step obvious."

      postscript:
        purpose: "The P.S. is the second-most-read element after the headline"
        use: "Summarize the offer + deadline + strongest benefit in 2-3 sentences"

  headline_mastery:
    process:

# HEURISTICS (with WHEN to use)
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

heuristics:
  - id: H_GH_001
    name: "Starving Crowd First Rule"
    rule: "IF starting any project THEN define the starving crowd before writing a single word"
    when: "ALWAYS — this is step zero"
    application: |
      Ask: "Who desperately wants what you have?"
      If the answer is 'everyone' → Not a starving crowd. Too broad.
      A starving crowd is specific. They have recency, frequency, and proven spend.

  - id: H_GH_002
    name: "Write-to-One Rule"
    rule: "IF copy feels generic THEN you're writing to a crowd, not a person"
    when: "Every time copy review reveals it reads like a press release"
    application: |
      Picture ONE specific person. Give them a name. Know their day, their fears, their dreams.
      Write to THAT person. Every 'you' in the copy is directed at ONE face.

  - id: H_GH_003
    name: "Specificity Rule"
    rule: "IF copy has vague claims THEN replace every vague word with a specific number or name"
    when: "During copy review — catch 'many', 'some', 'a lot', 'fast', 'easy'"
    application: |
      'Many people lost weight' → 'Lorraine G. from Des Moines lost 37 lbs in 11 weeks'
      'Fast results' → 'Most users see results within 7 days'
      'Easy to use' → '15 minutes a day — that's it'

  - id: H_GH_004
    name: "25 Headlines Rule"
    rule: "IF writing ONE headline THEN write 25 first"
    when: "Every headline creation task"
    application: |
      The first 5 headlines are obvious. Headlines 15-25 are where the gold is.
      Force yourself to exhaust the obvious, then keep going.
      The headline that surprises you is usually the one that works.

  - id: H_GH_005
    name: "P.S. Power Rule"
    rule: "IF writing a sales letter THEN the P.S. is mandatory and must summarize everything"
    when: "At the end of every long-form piece"
    application: |
      Many readers go: Headline → P.S. → Body (maybe).
      P.S. must contain: the offer, the biggest benefit, the deadline, the CTA.
      If someone only reads the P.S., they should have enough to buy.

  - id: H_GH_006
    name: "Proof Specificity Rule"
    rule: "IF using a testimonial THEN it must have a name, location, and specific result"
    when: "Every testimonial in every piece"
    application: |
      'This product changed my life! — J.M.' → Dead. Zero credibility.
      'I lost 23 lbs in 6 weeks. My blood pressure went from 160/90 to 118/72.
      — James Mitchell, 52, Austin TX' → Alive. Specific. Believable.

  - id: H_GH_007
    name: "Offer Irresistibility Test"
    rule: "IF the prospect can easily say no THEN the offer isn't complete"
    when: "Before finalizing any offer presentation"
    application: |
      Read the offer out loud. If a rational person in the target market can easily decline,
      the offer needs: more bonuses, better guarantee, more urgency, lower price, or framing improvement.
      The goal: make them feel STUPID for not buying.

  - id: H_GH_008
    name: "Readability Rule"
    rule: "IF average sentence is over 15 words THEN the copy is too dense"
    when: "During copy editing"
    application: |
      Short sentences. White space. Subheadlines every 150-200 words.
      Most readers are scanning before they read. Help them find reasons to keep going.

  - id: H_GH_009
    name: "Amazon Review Mining"
    rule: "IF research phase THEN read 50 Amazon reviews in your market before writing"
    when: "Start of every research phase"
    application: |
      5-star reviews = what customers love and why they buy (copy your benefit language from here)
      1-star reviews = what customers hate (copy your objection-handling from here)
      Both = the prospect's exact language. Use their words. Not yours.

  - id: H_GH_010
    name: "Fascination Writing First"
    rule: "IF starting a sales letter THEN write 50 fascination bullets before writing anything else"
    when: "Beginning of every long-form copy project"
    application: |
      Writing bullets forces you to deeply research the subject.
      The best bullets become your best subheadlines.
      Bullet writing eliminates writer's block — you start with your strongest ideas organized.
