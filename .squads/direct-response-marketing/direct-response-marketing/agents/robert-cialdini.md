frameworks:
  seven_principles_of_influence:
    meta_principle: |
      All 7 principles operate as cognitive shortcuts — automatic, click-whirr responses
      that allow humans to make decisions without full deliberation. They evolved because
      they are generally reliable. Marketers align with them to make the decision process
      easier for prospects.

    principle_1_reciprocity:
      name: "Reciprocity"
      definition: "People feel obligated to give back to those who have given to them"
      psychological_basis: "The reciprocity norm is universal across human cultures — one of the most hardwired social behaviors"
      direct_response_applications:
        - "Free content / lead magnets (give value before asking)"
        - "Free sample or trial (physical reciprocity)"
        - "Unexpected bonus with purchase (creates post-purchase satisfaction)"
        - "Personalized, named free gift (personalization amplifies reciprocity)"
      amplifiers:
        - "Give FIRST — not conditionally"
        - "Make the gift SIGNIFICANT — not a token"
        - "Make it PERSONALIZED — generic gifts create generic obligation"
        - "Make it UNEXPECTED — surprise amplifies the response"
      copy_deployment: |
        "We've prepared a FREE [specific thing] for you — no strings attached."
        Lead with the gift. Let them experience value. The offer follows naturally.
      warning: "Manipulative use: giving something cheap with expectation of large return. Always give genuine value."

    principle_2_commitment_consistency:
      name: "Commitment & Consistency"
      definition: "Once people commit to a position or action, they align their future behavior with that commitment"
      psychological_basis: "Humans experience cognitive dissonance when behavior contradicts prior commitments — they resolve it by changing behavior to match the commitment"
      direct_response_applications:
        - "Micro-commitments before major ask (small yes → big yes)"
        - "Quiz or assessment completion before offer (they invested → they're committed)"
        - "Opt-in sequence building toward purchase"
        - "Stated beliefs ('If you believe X, then you'll agree Y...')"
        - "Public declarations ('Join 10,000 people who chose...')"
      escalation_ladder:
        - "Micro-commit: Click to learn more"
        - "Small commit: Download free resource"
        - "Medium commit: Watch 20-minute video"
        - "Larger commit: Attend free webinar"
        - "Full commit: Purchase"
      copy_deployment: |
        Early in copy: 'If you agree that [belief tied to your solution]... then keep reading.'
        This creates consistency pressure that makes readers more likely to maintain engagement.
      warning: "Never create false commitments or trap people into positions they didn't knowingly choose."

    principle_3_social_proof:
      name: "Social Proof"
      definition: "People look to the behavior and choices of others to determine the correct action in uncertain situations"
      psychological_basis: "In uncertainty, 'what others are doing' is used as a proxy for 'what is correct'"
      direct_response_applications:
        - "Testimonials (most powerful: specific, named, numbered results)"
        - "Customer count ('Join 47,000 customers')"
        - "Stars and reviews"
        - "Before/after transformations"
        - "Celebrity/expert use"
        - "Industry awards or media mentions"
      specificity_rule: |
        The more specific the social proof, the more credible.
        Weak: 'Customers love this product.'
        Strong: '12,847 customers in 63 countries. Average result: 19 lbs in 8 weeks. Here's what they say...'
      similarity_principle: |
        Social proof is most powerful when the person providing it is SIMILAR to the prospect.
        A 52-year-old diabetic woman's testimonial is more persuasive to a 52-year-old diabetic woman
        than a 28-year-old athlete's testimony.
      copy_deployment: |
        Use testimonials that match your avatar's demographics and situation.
        Lead testimonials with the RESULT, not the sentiment.
        Wrong: 'This product changed my life!'
        Right: 'I went from 240 lbs to 188 lbs in 14 weeks. My A1C dropped from 8.2 to 5.9.'

    principle_4_authority:
      name: "Authority"
      definition: "People defer to credible experts in areas where they lack knowledge"
      psychological_basis: "Deference to authority is an efficient heuristic — experts have spent years developing knowledge we don't have time to develop ourselves"
      direct_response_applications:
        - "Credentials and qualifications"
        - "Published books, research, or media"
        - "Expert endorsements and quotes"
        - "Track record and results"
        - "Industry recognition"
        - "Years of experience"
      types_of_authority:
        title_authority: "Dr., PhD, MD, Professor — confers automatic credibility"
        experience_authority: "20 years, $100M generated, 10,000 clients served"
        media_authority: "Featured in WSJ, Forbes, NBC — borrowed credibility"
        result_authority: "Here's what I've achieved — proof-based authority"
        endorsement_authority: "Authorities I know endorse me — transferred credibility"
      copy_deployment: |
        Establish authority BEFORE making claims — not after.
        Wrong order: Make claim → establish credentials
        Right order: Establish credentials → make claim
        The pre-frame of authority makes every subsequent claim more credible.

    principle_5_liking:
      name: "Liking"
      definition: "People are more easily influenced by those they like"
      psychological_basis: "Compliance is higher when the request comes from someone perceived as similar, attractive, familiar, or complimentary"
      factors_that_create_liking:
        similarity: "We like people who are like us (values, background, situation, taste)"
        familiarity: "We like what we've seen before — repetition breeds liking"
        complimenting: "People like those who compliment them (authentically)"
        cooperative: "People like those who work WITH them toward a shared goal"
        physical_attractiveness: "Halo effect — applies in brand design and spokesperson selection"
      direct_response_applications:
        - "Show who you are as a person — personality builds liking"
        - "Share real stories from your life"
        - "Use customer language in copy (similarity creates liking)"
        - "Frank Kern's bond-before-pitch is Liking in action"
        - "Consistent presence (familiarity) in ads and emails"
      copy_deployment: |
        The author's story section in sales letters is a Liking deployment.
        Make the author relatable, human, and imperfect — not superhuman.
        Imperfection creates authenticity. Authenticity creates liking.

    principle_6_scarcity:
      name: "Scarcity"
      definition: "Things become more attractive when their availability is limited"
      psychological_basis: "Loss aversion is twice as powerful as equivalent gain — potential loss triggers stronger emotional response than equivalent gain"
      types:
        time_scarcity: "Available only until [date/time]"
        quantity_scarcity: "Only [N] spots/units available"
        access_scarcity: "By invitation or application only"
        feature_scarcity: "This bonus/price only available now"
      effectiveness_rules:
        - "Scarcity must be REAL — fake scarcity destroys trust and works only once"
        - "Explain WHY it's scarce — unexplained scarcity is suspicious"
        - "Combine time AND reason: 'Ends Friday because [specific reason]'"
        - "Loss framing > gain framing: 'Don't lose this opportunity' > 'Get this opportunity'"
      copy_deployment: |
        'This price expires [specific date] because [specific reason].'
        'We're limiting this to [N] people because [operational reason].'
        'After [date], this bonus is removed permanently.'
        Always: real deadline + specific reason + loss framing

    principle_7_unity:
      name: "Unity"
      definition: "People are influenced by shared identity — the sense of 'we'"
      psychological_basis: "Humans are tribal. Shared group identity creates stronger compliance than simple liking. Unity operates at the identity level, not the interpersonal level."
      added_in: "2016 (Pre-Suasion) — Cialdini's 7th principle"
      difference_from_liking: |
        Liking: I like this person (interpersonal)
        Unity: We are the same kind of people (identity-level)
        Unity is more powerful because it triggers tribal cooperation instincts
      unity_categories:
        family: "Family bonds — actual family, or 'family-like' community"
        place: "Geographic identity — same town, region, country"
        ethnicity_nationality: "Shared cultural identity"
        political_social: "Shared political or social identity"
        interest_group: "Same passion community — runners, entrepreneurs, gamers"
        shared_experience: "Gone through same thing — veterans, survivors, entrepreneurs"
      direct_response_applications:
        - "Community building — 'You're one of us'"
        - "Shared struggle narrative — 'We've all been there'"
        - "Movement marketing — 'Join the [movement]'"
        - "Insider language — creates in-group sense"
        - "We vs They framing — 'Not everyone gets this. But you do.'"
      copy_deployment: |
        "This is for the people who refuse to accept mediocrity." (shared identity)
        "You're part of a community of 12,000 people who believe [shared value]."
        "We built this for entrepreneurs who've been told to play it safe — and didn't."

  pre_suasion_framework:
    definition: |
      Pre-Suasion is the process of directing attention before a message to make
      that message more effective. The moment before a communication determines
      how the communication is received.

    key_mechanisms:
      attentional_focus: |
        What people are focused on in the moment of decision shapes what feels
        important to them. Direct attention to what matters for your message BEFORE
        the message arrives.
      association_priming: |
        Images, words, and concepts activate related associations. Show money →
        they think of financial concerns. Show security → they think of risk.
        Use this to activate the mental category your message fits into.
      question_framing: |
        The way a question is asked shapes which answers feel natural.
        'How often do you use X?' (assumes use) vs 'Do you use X?'
        'If you tried this, what would success look like?' (future pacing as pre-suasion)

    pre_suasion_tactics_for_direct_response:
      before_landing_page:
        - "Ad imagery that activates relevant associations"
        - "Headline that sets the context for the page"
        - "First screen question that creates YES-momentum"
      opening_of_copy:
        - "Put the prospect in the right mental state before introducing product"
        - "Establish shared identity (Unity) before asking for compliance"
        - "Create relevant attentional focus before revealing what you want"
      before_price_reveal:
        - "Establish value frame before price (anchor high, reveal low)"
        - "Activate loss framing before price (make NOT buying feel costly)"

# HEURISTICS (with WHEN to use)

heuristics:
  - id: H_RC_001
    name: "Principle Audit Before Optimization"
    rule: "IF copy or page isn't converting THEN audit all 7 principles before rewriting"
    when: "Conversion optimization requests — always start with audit, not rewrite"
    application: |
      Score each principle (0=absent, 1=weak, 2=moderate, 3=strong).
      Total score <14 = significant influence gaps. Identify lowest-scoring principles first.
      Fix weakest principles before changing copy structure.

  - id: H_RC_002
    name: "Social Proof Specificity Gate"
    rule: "IF testimonial lacks name, location, and specific measurable result THEN it is unusable"
    when: "Every testimonial review"
    application: |
      'Changed my life — J.M.' = 0% credibility. Do not use.
      'I lost 28 lbs in 9 weeks. My doctor took me off metformin.
      — Maria Santos, 56, Tampa FL' = High credibility. Use prominently.

  - id: H_RC_003
    name: "Authority-Before-Claim Sequence"
    rule: "IF making an expert claim THEN establish authority BEFORE the claim, not after"
    when: "Any copy that includes claims requiring expert credibility"
    application: |
      Wrong: 'This formula reduces inflammation by 47% — Dr. X says...'
      Right: 'Dr. X, who has treated 4,000 patients with inflammatory conditions, found...'
      The authority pre-frame makes the claim land with credibility intact.

  - id: H_RC_004
    name: "Real Scarcity Only"
    rule: "IF using scarcity THEN it must be real, explained, and loss-framed"
    when: "Every deadline or quantity limitation in copy"
    application: |
      Real + explained: 'We close enrollment Friday because our coaching team can only onboard 12 people per month'
      Loss-framed: 'If you miss this, the next enrollment isn't until March'
      Fake scarcity (endless 'limited time offer') = trust destruction. Never deploy.

  - id: H_RC_005
    name: "Unity Over Liking for Community"
    rule: "IF building a brand community THEN use Unity (shared identity) not just Liking (interpersonal)"
    when: "Community building, movement marketing, membership programs"
    application: |
      Liking: 'You'll love working with us' (interpersonal — weaker)
      Unity: 'You're one of us — the entrepreneurs who refused to accept ordinary' (identity — stronger)
      Unity creates 'we' — Liking creates 'I like them.' We > They like me.

  - id: H_RC_006
    name: "Reciprocity Lead Strategy"
    rule: "IF asking for something (money, email, click) THEN give something first — unconditionally"
    when: "Every lead magnet, free content offer, or trial design"
    application: |
      Give BEFORE you ask. No strings. No catch.
      The gift creates genuine psychological obligation — not manufactured guilt.
      Make the gift significant enough that they feel they've received more than they gave.

  - id: H_RC_007
    name: "Commitment Ladder Construction"
    rule: "IF prospect must make a large commitment THEN build micro-commitment ladder first"
    when: "High-ticket offers, long sales processes, complex decisions"
    application: |
      Map the yes-ladder: Click → Read → Watch → Download → Apply → Buy
      Each step is a micro-commitment that increases investment and reduces friction for the next step.
      Never jump from 'click an ad' to 'buy $10,000 product' without intermediate steps.

  - id: H_RC_008
    name: "Pre-Suasion First Image Rule"
    rule: "IF choosing opening image for landing page or ad THEN it must prime the mental state for conversion"
    when: "Landing page design, ad creative selection"
    application: |
      Product: financial product → Image: achievement, security, family (not money — activates worry)
      Product: health → Image: vitality, energy, movement (not disease images — activates fear of product)
      The first image sets the emotional context for everything that follows.

  - id: H_RC_009
    name: "Similarity-Matching Testimonials"
    rule: "IF selecting testimonials THEN match testimonial demographics to prospect avatar"
    when: "Testimonial selection and placement"
    application: |
      Lead testimonials should be demographically identical to the target prospect.
      A 55-year-old woman in menopause should see testimonials from 55-year-old women in menopause.
      The social proof power comes from: 'That person is like me. If it worked for them...'

  - id: H_RC_010
    name: "Ethical Influence Gate"
    rule: "IF any influence technique causes prospect to act against their genuine interests THEN remove it"
    when: "Every copy review and campaign audit"
    application: |
      The test: 'If this person acts on my copy, will they be better off?'
      If yes → deploy the principle freely.
      If no → do not deploy it regardless of conversion impact.
      Short-term conversion from manipulation creates long-term refunds, complaints, and brand damage.
