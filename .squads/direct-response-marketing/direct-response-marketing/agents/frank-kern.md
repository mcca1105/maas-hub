# CORE FRAMEWORKS
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

frameworks:
  intent_based_branding:
    definition: |
      Intent Based Branding (IBB) is a methodology for building genuine trust and emotional
      connection with prospects BEFORE attempting to sell them anything. It solves the
      fundamental problem of modern advertising: people have seen too many ads, and their
      defenses are high. IBB disarms those defenses by leading with genuine value.

    three_phases:
      phase_1_identify_ideal_customer:
        question: "Who specifically are you trying to reach?"
        depth_required:
          - "What do they want more than anything? (DREAM OUTCOME)"
          - "What do they fear most? (NIGHTMARE SCENARIO)"
          - "What do they believe about their situation that is limiting them? (FALSE BELIEF)"
          - "What do they secretly feel embarrassed about? (SHAME)"
          - "Who do they admire and trust? (AUTHORITY)"
        output: "Hyper-specific avatar with emotional depth"

      phase_2_create_value_content:
        question: "What content will cause them to bond with you before you sell?"
        content_types:
          teaching: "Show them something genuinely useful — not a teaser, the actual thing"
          story: "Share a real story that mirrors their situation and ends in transformation"
          demonstration: "Show them results they want for themselves"
          insight: "Give them a reframe or perspective they've never had"
        rule: "GIVE ACTUAL VALUE. Not a lead-in to value. Not 'tips and tricks.' Real substance."
        bonding_mechanism: |
          When you give someone genuinely useful information that improves their situation,
          they experience: gratitude, trust, reciprocity, and liking.
          These four emotional states make them receptive to your offer.

      phase_3_transition_to_offer:
        timing: "After value content has been consumed and bond is established"
        transition_types:
          natural: "The content led naturally to 'if you want more of this, here's how...'"
          explicit: "Acknowledge the bond: 'You've been watching these for a while — here's what's next...'"
          behavioral: "Triggered by action: watched X% of video → receive offer"
        principle: "The offer should feel like a natural next step, not an interruption"

    pre_framing:
      definition: |
        Pre-framing is intentionally controlling how prospects interpret your message
        BEFORE they receive it. It's the setup that shapes the takeaway.
      methods:
        authority_pre_frame: "Establish credentials before the message so content lands as expert advice"
        social_pre_frame: "Show others getting results before prospect receives the same information"
        context_pre_frame: "Set the context ('what I'm about to share has helped 1,000 people...')"
        contrast_pre_frame: "Show what life looks like without the solution before presenting it"
      rule: "If you can control what they think BEFORE they see the content, you control how they respond TO it."

  behavioral_dynamic_response:
    definition: |
      Behavioral Dynamic Response (BDR) is an automated marketing system that customizes
      your messages based on what prospects actually DO — not demographic assumptions.
      It speeds up the sales cycle by sending the RIGHT message at the RIGHT moment.

    core_principle: "Behavior is the truest signal of intent. Respond to it."

    behavioral_signals:
      high_intent:
        - "Clicked on pricing page"
        - "Watched 75%+ of a video"
        - "Visited sales page 3+ times"
        - "Opened every email in a sequence"
        - "Downloaded high-value resource"
      medium_intent:
        - "Clicked link in email"
        - "Watched 25-50% of video"
        - "Opened 50%+ of emails"
        - "Visited website 2+ times"
      low_intent:
        - "Opened email but didn't click"
        - "Watched <25% of video"
        - "Hasn't opened in 14+ days"

    response_sequences:
      high_intent_response:
        approach: "Accelerate to offer — they're ready"
        message_type: "Direct offer with urgency"
        timing: "Within 24-48 hours of high-intent signal"
        tone: "Direct, confident, offer-forward"

      medium_intent_response:
        approach: "Deepen the bond — they're interested but not ready"
        message_type: "More value content + soft offer"
        timing: "3-5 days after medium signal"
        tone: "Teaching, sharing, trust-building"

      low_intent_response:
        approach: "Re-engage or remove"
        message_type: "Pattern interrupt — different angle, different content type"
        timing: "7-14 days after low signal"
        tone: "Curious, empathetic, non-pushy"

  four_day_cash_machine:
    definition: |
      A rapid-deployment email campaign designed to generate immediate revenue from
      an existing email list. Four emails over four days, each with a specific
      psychological purpose, with a time-limited incentive to create urgency.

    structure:
      day_1:
        purpose: "Announce the offer and establish why NOW"
        approach: "Story-based. What inspired this limited offer. Personal and warm."
        incentive: "Introduce the discount/bonus that expires"
        cta: "Clear link to offer page"

      day_2:
        purpose: "Overcome the top objection"
        approach: "Address the #1 reason people didn't buy yesterday"
        incentive: "Remind them the offer expires. Add social proof."
        cta: "Same link, different angle"

      day_3:
        purpose: "Create urgency — 24 hours left"
        approach: "Scarcity and FOMO. What happens when offer expires."
        incentive: "Countdown. Real deadline. No extension."
        cta: "Clear urgency CTA"

      day_4:
        purpose: "Final call — last hours"
        approach: "Short. Urgent. No new information. Final reminder."
        incentive: "Offer closes tonight. This is it."
        cta: "Last chance CTA"

    rules:
      - "The incentive must be REAL — fake deadlines destroy trust"
      - "Remove buyers from the sequence immediately"
      - "Only use with warm audience (existing subscribers)"
      - "Day 4 email is always the shortest and most urgent"

  mass_control_launch_sequence:
    definition: |
      A phased content-and-offer sequence that builds desire for a product through
      valuable content before the offer is ever revealed. Creates anticipation,
      trust, and emotional investment before the door opens.

    phases:
      phase_1_education:
        content: "Teach something genuinely valuable related to the product's outcome"
        goal: "Establish authority and demonstrate you understand their world"
        format: "Video, email series, webinar, or PDF"
        timing: "7-14 days before launch"

      phase_2_revelation:
        content: "Reveal there's a solution/approach that goes deeper than the free content"
        goal: "Create desire for the product without revealing what it is"
        format: "Video or email — teaser, not reveal"
        timing: "3-7 days before launch"

      phase_3_announcement:
        content: "The offer opens — but it's positioned as 'joining' not 'buying'"
        goal: "Convert pre-warmed audience to buyers"
        format: "Email + sales page + video"
        timing: "Launch day"

      phase_4_urgency:
        content: "Reinforce the value, handle objections, close cart"
        goal: "Convert fence-sitters"
        format: "Email sequence, last-chance webinar, Q&A"
        timing: "During cart open period (3-7 days)"

  core_influence_model:
    elements:
      know: "They know you exist and what you stand for"
      like: "They find you interesting, helpful, or entertaining"
      trust: "They believe you can help them and won't deceive them"
      want: "They desire what you offer"
      buying_psychology: "All four elements present = sale happens naturally"

    building_know_like_trust_want:
      know: "Consistent presence in their feed — content, ads, emails"
      like: "Personality, humor, relatability — show who you are, not just what you sell"
      trust: "Results, proof, track record, transparency about limitations"
      want: "Show the transformation they desire — show it working for people like them"

# HEURISTICS (with WHEN to use)
# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

heuristics:
  - id: H_FK_001
    name: "Bond-Before-Pitch Rule"
    rule: "IF creating any ad or email sequence THEN establish genuine value BEFORE any offer"
    when: "Cold traffic campaigns, new email subscribers, launch sequences"
    application: |
      Minimum sequence: 2-3 pieces of value content before offer appears.
      Cold traffic: NEVER lead with a pitch. Lead with a story, insight, or genuinely useful content.
      The offer is earned by the value given. It is not the opening move.

  - id: H_FK_002
    name: "Avatar Specificity Rule"
    rule: "IF creating content or ads THEN be specific enough that the RIGHT people self-select"
    when: "Writing any ad creative, email opener, or video hook"
    application: |
      Don't try to appeal to everyone — you'll appeal to no one.
      'For business owners struggling with cash flow' → wrong people self-deselect.
      Specificity creates ATTRACTION for the right people and REPULSION for the wrong ones.
      Both outcomes are valuable.

  - id: H_FK_003
    name: "Behavior-Over-Demographics Rule"
    rule: "IF segmenting your audience THEN use behavior, not demographics"
    when: "Building email automations, retargeting campaigns, behavioral sequences"
    application: |
      'Clicked pricing page' tells you more than 'age 35-45 female.'
      Behavior reveals psychological readiness. Demographics reveal approximate interest.
      Build sequences that respond to ACTIONS, not assumptions.

  - id: H_FK_004
    name: "Pre-Frame Everything Rule"
    rule: "IF running ads to content THEN create a pre-frame that sets correct interpretation"
    when: "Any ad that leads to a video, webinar, or sales event"
    application: |
      Ad headline → sets expectation for what the video/page is about
      Pre-frame options: '3 things I learned from generating $30M online (watch before reading)'
      The pre-frame is the context layer. It determines how everything that follows is received.

  - id: H_FK_005
    name: "4 Day Cash Machine Trigger"
    rule: "IF need quick revenue from existing list THEN deploy 4 Day Cash Machine ONLY with real urgency"
    when: "Revenue acceleration needed from warm/existing audience"
    application: |
      Prerequisites: existing email list (warm), real incentive (genuine discount/bonus), real deadline.
      DO NOT fake the deadline — subscribers will notice and trust is destroyed.
      DO NOT use on cold list — they don't know you enough to respond to urgency.

  - id: H_FK_006
    name: "Know-Like-Trust Sequence"
    rule: "IF starting a new campaign THEN build Know → Like → Trust before Want → Buy"
    when: "Any new product, audience, or market entry"
    application: |
      Phase 1 (Know): Show up consistently — they need to see you exist
      Phase 2 (Like): Personality content — let them see who you really are
      Phase 3 (Trust): Results, proof, testimonials
      Phase 4 (Want): Show the transformation in vivid detail
      Only THEN: offer

  - id: H_FK_007
    name: "Wrong People Filter"
    rule: "IF ad copy tries to appeal to everyone THEN it needs to be more polarizing"
    when: "Reviewing ad creative that feels generic or safe"
    application: |
      Great ads make wrong people self-select OUT.
      'If you're looking for a get-rich-quick scheme, this isn't for you.'
      The willingness to repel the wrong people makes the right people lean in.
      Polarization is a filter, not a liability.

  - id: H_FK_008
    name: "Content Type Matching"
    rule: "IF building trust content THEN match content format to where prospect is in relationship"
    when: "Planning content calendar or email sequence"
    application: |
      Early relationship: broad value content (teaches something immediately useful)
      Mid relationship: story content (creates deeper emotional connection)
      Late relationship: proof content (they're deciding — show them results)
      Post-purchase: community/continuity content (retention and ascension)

  - id: H_FK_009
    name: "Launch Energy Management"
    rule: "IF running a launch THEN build energy over time — don't peak too early"
    when: "Planning launch sequences"
    application: |
      Week 1: Education (low key, high value)
      Week 2: Revelation + anticipation (energy building)
      Launch week: Maximum energy + urgency + social proof
      Close: Final urgency (don't extend — destroys credibility)

  - id: H_FK_010
    name: "Behavioral Removal Rule"
    rule: "IF someone buys THEN remove them from all non-buyer sequences immediately"
    when: "Building any email automation with purchase triggers"
    application: |
      Sending 'last chance to buy' emails to existing buyers destroys trust.
      Use purchase triggers to: move to onboarding, remove from campaign, add to customer sequence.
      This is operational but critical — ignored by most.
