# Task: Write Direct Mail Letter

**Agent:** gary-halbert
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Creation
**Elicit:** true

## Purpose
Write a complete physical direct mail letter — from envelope teaser through PS.
Gary Halbert's principle: the best copywriting in the world lives in physical mail.
The reader is not distracted, not multitasking — they're holding something in their hands.
This is the most intimate marketing medium that exists.

## Prerequisites
- List profile defined (who is receiving this letter?)
- Offer complete (structure-irresistible-offer.md)
- Budget and mailing specifications confirmed (letter size, postage type, response mechanism)

## Inputs Required
- List description (existing customers, purchased list, warm referrals)
- Product and offer to promote
- Response mechanism (phone, URL, BRC — Business Reply Card)
- Deadline for mailing to printer
- Per-piece budget (determines package complexity)

## The Direct Mail Package Components

A complete direct mail package contains:
1. **Outer envelope** — the gateway. If this fails, nothing else matters.
2. **Letter** — the main copy. Can be 1-12 pages.
3. **Lift note** — optional short note from a different voice ("a note from our president")
4. **BRC/Response device** — the physical coupon or form they return
5. **Reply envelope** — if response by mail

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Outer Envelope Strategy

The decision to open the envelope is the first sale.

**Teaser copy options:**

**A — Curiosity (no teaser):**
Blank envelope with just the address — curiosity drives opening.
Best for: Highly targeted lists where the name on the envelope is enough

**B — Benefit Teaser:**
"Inside: How to save $4,200 on your taxes before April 15th"
Best for: Known audience with known pain point

**C — News/Urgency:**
"Time-sensitive information enclosed — please open immediately"
Best for: Financial, legal, health markets

**D — Personalization:**
"[First Name], your complimentary report is enclosed"
Best for: Warmer lists with existing relationship

**E — Social Proof:**
"Used by 47,000 homeowners in [State]"
Best for: Local or regional campaigns with strong social proof

Select teaser strategy based on list temperature and budget.

### Step 2: The Letter Opening — The Lead

The first paragraph determines if the letter is read. Gary Halbert's rule:
"The lead must be so compelling that you feel physically unable to stop reading."

**Lead formulas:**

**A — Story Lead:**
Drop the reader into a scene. No preamble.
"It was 11pm on a Tuesday when my phone rang. I almost didn't answer it."

**B — Provocative Question:**
"What would you do if your doctor told you that the one thing you're doing to improve your health is actually causing the problem?"

**C — Startling Statistic:**
"87% of Americans are unknowingly overpaying their taxes by an average of $4,200 per year. Are you one of them?"

**D — You Lead (Reader-Focused):**
"If you're a homeowner in [City] who has ever worried about [problem], then this letter is the most important thing you'll read this week."

**E — Offer Lead (for hot lists):**
"I'll get straight to the point: for the next [X] days, I'm making an offer I've never made before. And it's only for people on this list."

### Step 3: Letter Structure

**The Salutation:**
Always as personal as the data allows:
- "Dear [First Name]," — ideal (requires first name in data)
- "Dear Fellow [Avatar Identity]," — good (Dear Fellow Homeowner, Dear Fellow Nurse)
- "Dear Friend," — acceptable (Gary Halbert used this when personalization wasn't possible)
- NEVER: "To Whom It May Concern" or "Dear Valued Customer"

**The Body — Follow This Flow:**

**(1) Problem Confirmation (1-2 paragraphs):**
Name the problem the reader is experiencing. Validate their frustration.
Use their language. Be specific.

**(2) Amplification (2-3 paragraphs):**
Make the consequences of not solving the problem vivid and real.
The cost in money, time, health, relationships, missed opportunities.

**(3) Credibility / Why Me (1-2 paragraphs):**
Why should they listen to you specifically?
Track record. Background. Who you've helped. What you've done.

**(4) The Solution Introduction (1-2 paragraphs):**
Introduce the mechanism. What is it? Why does it work when other things don't?

**(5) Proof (2-4 paragraphs or testimonial block):**
Evidence the solution works:
- Testimonials (FULL NAME + CITY — never just initials)
- Specific results with numbers
- Before and after descriptions

**(6) The Offer (2-3 paragraphs):**
What exactly are they getting?
List every component with its value.
Show the anchor price vs. the offer price.

**(7) The Guarantee:**
Gary Halbert's principle: "Make the guarantee so strong that you'd be embarrassed not to stand behind it."
Name the guarantee. Describe what they need to do to use it.

**(8) Urgency and Deadline:**
Every letter must have a specific deadline.
The reason must be real ("We can only service 47 new clients per quarter" or "This pricing expires December 31st").

**(9) The CTA:**
Exactly what to do RIGHT NOW.
"Call 1-800-XXX-XXXX and mention code [X]"
Or: "Complete the enclosed reply card and mail it in the prepaid envelope"
Or: "Visit [URL] or call [number] by [date]"

### Step 4: Write the PS

Gary Halbert's most important rule about the PS:
**The PS is often read BEFORE the letter body.**
Eye-tracking research confirms: readers scan to the signature, then read the PS.

**The PS must contain:**
- The complete offer summary
- The deadline
- The CTA

Even someone who reads ONLY the PS should know exactly what's being offered and what to do.

"P.S. Remember — this offer expires [specific date]. Call [number] today and mention code [X] to receive [Product Name] for just $[price], plus [bonus], backed by our [X]-day guarantee."

### Step 5: The BRC / Response Card

The Business Reply Card is the physical action device. Copy rules:

- Checkbox format: "YES! I want to [specific outcome]. Please send me [Product Name] for just $[price]."
- Repeat the guarantee on the card: "I understand I'm protected by your [X]-day guarantee"
- Payment options (credit card, check, money order) with fields
- Prominent space for name, address, phone, email
- Reference code for tracking this mailing
- Prepaid postage (BRC format) or instructions to return

## Quality Standards
- Teaser on envelope creates irresistible curiosity or relevant urgency
- Salutation is as personal as data allows
- Lead is riveting — can't stop reading
- Every testimonial has full name + city (no "J.D. from California")
- PS contains complete offer + deadline + CTA
- BRC makes response frictionless
- Tracking code present for ROI measurement

## Veto Conditions
- VETO if salutation is "To Whom It May Concern" or "Dear Valued Customer"
- VETO if letter has no PS
- VETO if PS doesn't contain the full offer and CTA
- VETO if no deadline in the letter
- VETO if testimonials use only initials or lack specific results
- VETO if no tracking mechanism (can't measure which list/mailing drove response)
- VETO if response device makes responding complicated (too many fields, no prepaid postage)
