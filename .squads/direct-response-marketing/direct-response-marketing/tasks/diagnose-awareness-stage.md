# Task: Diagnose Awareness Stage

**Agent:** eugene-schwartz
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Diagnostic
**Elicit:** true

## Purpose
Identify the exact awareness stage (1-5) of the target prospect before any copy is written.
This is the mandatory first step for all copy projects.

## Inputs Required
- Product/offer category
- Target prospect description
- Competitive landscape (what competitors are advertising)
- Any existing market research

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Market Landscape Review
Assess what prospects have already been exposed to:
- How long has this category existed?
- How many competitors are actively advertising?
- What claims are saturating the market?

### Step 2: Awareness Stage Diagnosis
Apply Schwartz's 5-stage framework:

**Stage 1 — Completely Unaware**
Signal: Prospects don't know they have this problem
Test: Would a random person in this demographic recognize the problem if described?
→ If NO → Stage 1

**Stage 2 — Problem Aware**
Signal: They know they have a problem but don't know solutions exist
Test: Do they search for symptoms rather than solutions?
→ If YES → Stage 2

**Stage 3 — Solution Aware**
Signal: They know solutions exist but not your specific solution
Test: Have they tried competitors? Do they say 'nothing works'?
→ If YES → Stage 3

**Stage 4 — Product Aware**
Signal: They know your product but aren't convinced
Test: Can they name your brand or product category?
→ If YES → Stage 4

**Stage 5 — Most Aware**
Signal: Ready to buy, just needs the right trigger
Test: Are they on your email list, warm traffic, or past visitors?
→ If YES → Stage 5

### Step 3: Market Sophistication Level
Assess sophistication (1-5):
- Level 1: First to market with this claim
- Level 2: Some competition using similar benefits
- Level 3: Market jaded with generic claims — mechanism required
- Level 4: Mechanisms proliferating — need specific proof
- Level 5: Only identity and community work

### Step 4: Copy Strategy Recommendation
Based on stage and sophistication level, output:
- Recommended copy opening strategy
- Headline approach
- Content length
- Proof requirements
- Mechanism requirement (yes/no)

