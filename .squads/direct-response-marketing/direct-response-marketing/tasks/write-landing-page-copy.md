# Task: Write Landing Page Copy

**Agent:** ryan-deiss
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Creation
**Elicit:** true

## Purpose
Write complete landing page copy — from headline through CTA — calibrated to the
temperature of the incoming traffic. Ryan Deiss's principle: the landing page must
pick up exactly where the traffic source left off, with perfect message-to-market match.

## Prerequisites
- Avatar defined (create-customer-avatar.md)
- Traffic source identified (where are they coming from?)
- Offer defined (structure-irresistible-offer.md)
- Awareness stage diagnosed

## Inputs Required
- Traffic source (Facebook cold, Google search, email list, YouTube, retargeting)
- Page type (opt-in, sales, webinar registration, VSL page)
- Offer details (product, bonuses, price, guarantee)
- Proof elements available
- Mechanism or unique angle

## Traffic Temperature Framework

**Cold Traffic (strangers — Facebook cold, native, YouTube):**
- They don't know you
- They may not fully recognize the problem
- They need: problem agitation FIRST, then mechanism, then offer
- Page structure: problem → mechanism → credibility → offer

**Warm Traffic (aware of problem — Google search, retargeting):**
- They're actively looking for solutions
- They may know you slightly
- They need: solution positioning, differentiation, proof
- Page structure: promise → differentiation → proof → offer

**Hot Traffic (email list, retargeting with high engagement):**
- They know you and your content
- They may already want what you're selling
- They need: specific offer details, urgency, easy decision
- Page structure: offer → value stack → social proof → CTA

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Traffic Source Analysis
Determine the exact state of mind of the visitor when they arrive:

```yaml
traffic_profile:
  source: "[Facebook ad / Google search / Email / etc.]"
  temperature: "[cold/warm/hot]"
  what_they_just_saw: "[The ad/email/video that sent them here]"
  what_they_expect: "[What did that content promise?]"
  awareness_stage: "[1-5]"
  primary_objection: "[Most likely reason to not convert]"
```

The copy on the landing page MUST be a logical continuation of what they just consumed.
Disconnect between ad and landing page = high bounce rates.

### Step 2: Above-the-Fold Architecture
Everything visible without scrolling must accomplish one job: make them want to scroll.

**For cold traffic page (opt-in or VSL):**
```
[Pattern interrupt visual or bold statement]
[HEADLINE — Problem or curiosity-based]
[SUBHEADLINE — Expand the promise, hint at mechanism]
[Short bullet list: 3 things they'll discover]
[CTA button #1 — positioned here]
[Trust signal: "As seen in..." or number social proof]
```

**For warm traffic sales page:**
```
[HEADLINE — Bold promise with specific outcome]
[SUBHEADLINE — How you achieve it differently]
[Hero image or product image]
[3 key benefits as icons/bullets]
[CTA button #1]
[Trust bar: logos, certifications, guarantee badge]
```

**For hot traffic (email or retargeting):**
```
[HEADLINE — Specific to the offer being promoted]
[SUBHEADLINE — Key differentiator or urgency element]
[Direct CTA — they already know you, get to the point]
[Quick proof (testimonial or result)]
```

### Step 3: Write the Headline
The headline is the first test — if it fails, nothing else matters.

**Generate 5 headline variations:**

**Variation A — Specific Result + Timeframe:**
"[Specific Result] in [Timeframe] — Even If [Biggest Objection]"
→ "Generate Your First $10,000 Month in 90 Days — Even With Zero Audience"

**Variation B — Problem Identification:**
"Finally: The [Mechanism] That [Solves Problem] Without [Painful Sacrifice]"
→ "Finally: The Blood Sugar Protocol That Works Without Giving Up Every Food You Love"

**Variation C — Curiosity/Discovery:**
"The [Counterintuitive Insight] That [Avatar] Uses to [Result]"
→ "The 'Backwards' Marketing Method That 6-Figure Coaches Use to Fill Calendars Without Cold DMs"

**Variation D — Fear/Warning:**
"WARNING: If You're Still [Common Mistake], You're [Negative Consequence]"

**Variation E — Social Proof:**
"[Number] [Avatars] Have Used This to [Specific Result] — Here's Exactly How"

Select primary headline. Write split-test backup.

### Step 4: Page Sections (Full Structure)

**Section 1 — Problem (Cold only, Warm sometimes):**
Validate the problem they're experiencing. Make them feel seen.
- Name the problem specifically (use their language from VOC research)
- Describe the frustration of failed solutions
- Position the root cause (hint at mechanism)

**Section 2 — Mechanism Introduction:**
Introduce the unique mechanism — WHY your solution works differently:
- Name it specifically
- Explain simply (what it is + why it works)
- Differentiate from what they've tried

**Section 3 — Proof Stack:**
Build credibility before the offer:
- 2-3 testimonials (specific: name, location, result, timeframe)
- Data point or study if available
- Authority/credibility signal (credentials, media logos, results)

**Section 4 — Offer Presentation:**
Present the offer only AFTER proof is established:
- Core product description + components
- Bonus stack (each bonus with specific value)
- Total value calculation (anchoring)
- Price reveal with framing
- Guarantee (positioned as confidence, not desperation)

**Section 5 — Urgency/Scarcity:**
Real reason to act now:
- Deadline with specific date and reason
- OR quantity limit with reason
- Consequence of waiting (what they miss or lose)

**Section 6 — Final CTA:**
Repeat the primary CTA with:
- Summary of what they get
- Reminder of guarantee
- Urgency reiterated
- CTA button with specific action text

### Step 5: CTA Copy
CTA button text is often the most tested element:

**Weak CTAs (avoid):**
- "Submit"
- "Sign Up"
- "Buy Now"
- "Learn More"

**Strong CTAs (use):**
- "Yes, I Want [Specific Result]!"
- "Start My [Timeframe] to [Outcome] Now"
- "Get Instant Access to [Product Name]"
- "Claim My [Bonus] Before [Deadline]"
- "Show Me How to [Specific Action]"

### Step 6: Copy Below the Fold
Elements that support the conversion after the initial hook:

- **The Story Section:** Relatable origin story (who created this and why)
- **FAQ Section:** Pre-emptively answer the 5 most common hesitations
- **Testimonial Wall:** Multiple testimonials from different avatar types
- **Comparison Section:** Side-by-side vs. competitors or alternatives (optional)
- **Guarantee Block:** Large, prominent, reassuring

## Quality Standards
- Message-match: first line of page continues directly from traffic source
- Specificity: every claim has a number, name, or timeframe
- CTA appears minimum 3 times on the page (above fold, mid-page, bottom)
- Testimonials are specific (not "Great product!")
- Guarantee is prominently displayed, easy to find
- Mobile version reviewed (above fold works on 375px viewport)

## Veto Conditions
- VETO if headline is generic (could be used by any competitor)
- VETO if there is no CTA above the fold
- VETO if offer is presented before any proof
- VETO if testimonials are vague or lack specifics
- VETO if cold traffic page leads with product features (not problem/benefit)
- VETO if guarantee is buried or hard to find
