# Task: Write Squeeze Page

**Agent:** gary-halbert
**Tier:** 2
**Type:** Creation
**Elicit:** true

## Purpose
Write a high-converting squeeze page that captures email leads in exchange for a
lead magnet. Gary Halbert's principle: the squeeze page is the first transaction in
the relationship — make it feel like a gift, not a trap.

## Prerequisites
- Lead magnet created and ready to deliver (create-lead-magnet.md)
- Avatar defined (create-customer-avatar.md)
- Traffic source identified (determines how much copy is needed)

## Inputs Required
- Lead magnet name and description
- Traffic source (cold Facebook, warm retargeting, search, social organic)
- Traffic temperature (cold needs more copy, warm needs less)
- What happens after opt-in (immediate redirect, thank you page, email sequence)

## The Squeeze Page Logic

A squeeze page has ONE job: trade the lead magnet for the email address.
There is no second offer, no navigation menu, no links out.

**Traffic temperature → copy length:**
- Cold traffic: 200-400 words (need to explain who you are and why the LM is valuable)
- Warm traffic: 100-200 words (they already know you — get to the point)
- Hot traffic: 50-100 words (they want what you're offering — don't slow them down)

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Lead Magnet Positioning
Before writing, crystallize the lead magnet's value:

```yaml
lead_magnet_profile:
  name: "[Exact name of lead magnet]"
  format: "[Checklist/PDF/video/template/tool/mini-course]"
  delivery_time: "[Immediate download / video access / etc.]"
  core_promise: "[What specific result or insight do they get?]"
  time_to_consume: "[5 minutes / 1 hour / etc.]"
  key_benefits:
    - "[Benefit 1 — specific]"
    - "[Benefit 2 — specific]"
    - "[Benefit 3 — specific]"
  biggest_skepticism: "[Why would they doubt this is worth their email?]"
```

### Step 2: Write the Headline
The headline is 80% of the squeeze page. If it fails, the form never gets filled.

**5 headline formats to test:**

**A — How-To + Specific Result:**
"How to [Achieve Specific Result] in [Timeframe] — [Lead Magnet Name]"
→ "How to Write a High-Converting Email in 15 Minutes — The 7-Step Template"

**B — The Secret/Method:**
"The [Number]-Step [Method Name] That [Specific Avatars] Use to [Specific Result]"
→ "The 3-Part Framework That Freelance Writers Use to Land $5,000 Clients Without a Portfolio"

**C — What You'll Discover:**
"Discover How to [Result] Even If [Biggest Objection]"
→ "Discover How to Lose 10 Pounds in 30 Days Even If You've Failed at Every Diet Before"

**D — Warning/Problem:**
"WARNING: [Common Approach] Is [Negative Consequence] — Here's What to Do Instead [FREE]"
→ "WARNING: Your Email Subject Lines Are Killing Your Open Rates — Here Are 47 That Work"

**E — Social Proof:**
"[Number] [Avatars] Are Using This [Lead Magnet Name] to [Specific Result]"
→ "14,000+ Small Business Owners Are Using This Tax Checklist to Save an Average of $4,200/Year"

### Step 3: Write the Subheadline
2-3 sentences that expand the headline and handle the primary objection:

Format: "In this free [format], you'll discover [specific insight/tool/method]. [Overcome primary objection]. No [common sacrifice] required."

Example: "In this free 12-page guide, you'll discover the exact email templates that generated $230,000 in 90 days. No copywriting experience required — just fill in the blanks."

### Step 4: Write the Bullet Points (3-5 bullets)
Bullets tease specific insights without giving them away. Each bullet creates desire for one specific thing inside the lead magnet.

**Formula:** "The [specific thing] that [specific result or outcome]"
Or: "How to [specific action] — even if [specific objection]"
Or: "[Number] [items] that [outcome]"

**Never:** Generic bullets ("Tips and strategies for success")
**Always:** Specific secrets ("The exact subject line that got a 64% open rate — and the 3-word principle behind it")

### Step 5: Write the Form and CTA Button
The form itself communicates value through what it asks:

**Ask for minimum required:**
- Best: Email only (highest conversion)
- Good: First name + email
- Avoid: More than 2 fields (significant drop in conversion)

**CTA button copy:**
The button is the final nudge. These words matter enormously.

**Weak buttons (avoid):**
- "Submit"
- "Sign Up"
- "Subscribe"
- "Send it to me"

**Strong buttons (use):**
- "Yes! Send Me The [Lead Magnet Name] Now →"
- "Get Instant Access to [Specific Benefit] →"
- "Show Me The [Method/Formula/System] →"
- "Send My Free [Lead Magnet] →"
- "[First-person confirmation]: "I want to [result] — send it now →"

### Step 6: Write the Privacy/Trust Line
Below the form, one line of reassurance:

Format: "Your information is 100% secure. We never share or sell your email. Unsubscribe anytime."

Or for niche-specific trust: "We hate spam as much as you do. Your email is safe with us."

### Step 7: Optional Elements for Cold Traffic
For cold traffic squeeze pages, add below the form:

**Proof of relevance:**
- "[Number] people have already downloaded this" (social proof of demand)
- "[Media logo] featured" (third-party credibility)
- One micro-testimonial: "This checklist saved me 8 hours a week. — [Name], [Location]"

**Who this is for:**
Brief description of exactly who the lead magnet serves — helps filter qualified leads:
"This is for [specific avatar description]. If you're [characteristic], this is for you."

## Quality Standards
- Page has zero navigation links or exit opportunities (squeeze page = no exits)
- CTA button is the most prominent element after the headline
- Lead magnet format and delivery method are clear
- Bullets are specific (never generic)
- Mobile-optimized (form works perfectly on mobile)
- Page loads in under 3 seconds

## Veto Conditions
- VETO if page has navigation menu or external links (kills conversion)
- VETO if CTA button says "Submit" or "Sign Up"
- VETO if headline is generic (could apply to any lead magnet)
- VETO if bullets are vague tips ("Learn the secrets to success")
- VETO if more than 3 form fields requested
- VETO if what happens after opt-in is unclear
