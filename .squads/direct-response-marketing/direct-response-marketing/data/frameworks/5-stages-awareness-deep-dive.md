# Eugene Schwartz - 5 Stages of Awareness Deep Dive

## The Foundation of All Great Copy

Eugene Schwartz's "Breakthrough Advertising" (1966) introduced the concept that revolutionized copywriting: **Awareness Stages**.

**Core Insight:** "Copy cannot create desire. It can only tap into existing desire and channel it toward your product."

---

## The 5 Stages

```
UNAWARE → PROBLEM AWARE → SOLUTION AWARE → PRODUCT AWARE → MOST AWARE
  (Cold)      (Warming)        (Warm)          (Hot)       (Hottest)
```

---

## Stage 1: UNAWARE (Completely Cold Traffic)

### Who They Are:
- Don't know they have a problem
- Not looking for any solution
- Completely cold to your market

### What They Need:
- Education about the problem
- Wake-up call
- Pattern interrupt

### Copy Approach:

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Sell your product directly
- ❌ Assume they know they have a problem
- ❌ Use industry jargon

**DO:**
- ✅ Tell a story that reveals the problem
- ✅ Ask questions that make them think
- ✅ Use pattern interrupts
- ✅ Focus on their world, not your product

### Example Headlines:

```
❌ BAD (Selling Too Soon):
"Buy Our Weight Loss Supplement - 50% Off!"

✅ GOOD (Problem Discovery):
"Are You Making These 7 Mistakes That Slow Your Metabolism?"

"The Silent Inflammation That's Adding Pounds While You Sleep"

"Why 92% of Diets Fail (And What Harvard Scientists Just Discovered)"
```

### Content Structure:

**1. Pattern Interrupt**
```
You've been lied to about weight loss.

It's not your fault you can't lose weight.

And it has nothing to do with willpower, calories, or exercise.

In fact, the harder you try, the worse it gets.

Here's why...
```

**2. Problem Education**
```
New research from Harvard Medical School reveals that [specific problem]
is sabotaging weight loss for millions of Americans.

This explains why:
• You can eat perfectly and still gain weight
• Your energy crashes every afternoon
• You feel hungry 2 hours after eating

The culprit? [Problem introduced]
```

**3. Story That Relates**
```
Let me tell you about Sarah, a 42-year-old mother who tried everything...

[Relatable story that shows the hidden problem]

The shocking discovery? [Problem revealed]
```

**4. Call to Action (Education, Not Sale)**
```
Want to know if [problem] is affecting you?

Download our free report: "The 7 Hidden Signs of [Problem]"

[Link to report]

Inside, you'll discover:
• How to tell if you have [problem]
• What triggers it
• 3 simple tests you can do at home

No cost. No strings attached. Just information you need.
```

### Conversion Goal:
Move them from **Unaware → Problem Aware**

## Stage 2: PROBLEM AWARE (They Know They Have a Problem)

### Who They Are:
- Aware they have a problem/pain
- Actively looking for information
- Don't know solutions exist (or which one is right)

### What They Need:
- Understanding of the problem
- Hope that solutions exist
- Education about solution types

### Copy Approach:

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Jump straight to your product
- ❌ Assume they know about solutions
- ❌ Use features and specs

**DO:**
- ✅ Agitate the problem (empathize)
- ✅ Introduce the concept of a solution
- ✅ Present your mechanism as THE solution type
- ✅ Build desire for the mechanism

### Example Headlines:

```
❌ BAD (Jumping to Product):
"Our CRM Software Saves You 10 Hours Per Week"

✅ GOOD (Problem Agitation + Solution Introduction):
"Losing Customers Because You Can't Keep Up With Follow-Ups?
Here's The Solution Fortune 500 Companies Use"

"The Real Reason You're Overwhelmed (And The Simple System That Fixes It)"

"How 12,000+ Small Businesses Solved The 'Too Many Leads, Not Enough Time'
Problem"
```

### Content Structure:

**1. Problem Agitation (Make It Real)**
```
You know the feeling...

A hot lead comes in.

You're excited. You respond quickly.

They're interested.

You schedule a follow-up.

Then... life happens.

More leads come in. Phone rings. Emails pile up.

Three weeks later, you remember that hot lead.

You follow up. Too late. They went with someone else.

It happens again. And again. And again.

You're not lazy. You're not disorganized.

You're just overwhelmed.

And every missed follow-up is money walking out the door.

Sound familiar?
```

**2. The Bigger Picture (Not Your Fault)**
```
Here's what nobody tells you:

This isn't a "you" problem. It's a systems problem.

You can't keep every lead in your head.
You can't remember every follow-up.
You can't manually track every conversation.

Your brain isn't designed for this.

That's why 67% of sales are lost simply because salespeople don't follow up.

Not because they don't care. Because they're human.

You need a system. Not willpower.
```

**3. Solution Introduction (The Mechanism)**
```
This is where a CRM (Customer Relationship Management system) comes in.

Think of it as a "second brain" for your business.

It automatically:
• Reminds you to follow up at the perfect time
• Tracks every conversation
• Organizes your leads
• Shows you exactly who needs attention

Fortune 500 companies use CRMs to manage millions of customers.

Now, small businesses can use the same technology.

And it's easier than you think.
```

**4. Proof That Solution Works**
```
Since implementing a CRM, our clients report:

• 35% increase in closed deals (same leads, better follow-up)
• 10+ hours saved per week (no more manual tracking)
• Zero leads falling through the cracks

"I thought I needed to hire someone to handle follow-ups.
Turns out I just needed a system. This paid for itself in the first month."
- Mike R., Real Estate Agent
```

**5. Call to Action (Learn More About Solution Type)**
```
Want to see how a CRM could transform your business?

Download our free guide:
"The Small Business Owner's Guide to CRMs: How to Never Lose a Lead Again"

Inside you'll learn:
• What a CRM actually does (in plain English)
• How to choose the right one for your business
• The 3 features that matter most
• Real ROI calculations from similar businesses

[Download Free Guide]

No tech jargon. No sales pitch. Just practical information.
```

### Conversion Goal:
Move them from **Problem Aware → Solution Aware**

## Stage 3: SOLUTION AWARE (They Know Solutions Exist)

### Who They Are:
- Know their problem
- Know solutions exist
- Researching options
- Comparing approaches

### What They Need:
- Why YOUR approach is superior
- Proof it works
- Trust and credibility

### Copy Approach:

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Assume they'll pick you automatically
- ❌ Talk only about features
- ❌ Ignore competitive alternatives

**DO:**
- ✅ Acknowledge alternatives
- ✅ Explain why your mechanism is better
- ✅ Provide comparison criteria
- ✅ Show proof and results

### Example Headlines:

```
✅ "CRMs vs Spreadsheets vs Pen & Paper: The Honest Comparison
(And Why 87% of Growing Businesses Choose Option #1)"

✅ "Free CRMs vs Paid CRMs: What You're Really Giving Up
(And When It Matters)"

✅ "The 3 Types of CRM Systems - Which One Will Actually Get Used
By Your Team?"
```

### Content Structure:

**1. Validate Their Research**
```
Smart move researching CRMs before buying.

There are hundreds of options, and choosing the wrong one costs you
time and money.

The good news? There are really only 3 types that matter:

1. Enterprise CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot) - Powerful but overwhelming
2. Simple CRMs (Us, Pipedrive) - Easy but capable
3. DIY Solutions (Spreadsheets) - Free but manual

Here's how to choose...
```

**2. Comparison (Acknowledge Alternatives)**
```
ENTERPRISE CRMs ($$$):
✅ Extremely powerful
✅ Infinite customization
❌ Steep learning curve (months)
❌ Expensive ($100-300/mo per user)
❌ Requires dedicated admin
BEST FOR: Teams of 50+ with IT support

SIMPLE CRMs ($$):
✅ Quick setup (minutes, not months)
✅ Team actually uses it (intuitive)
✅ Affordable ($20-50/mo total)
❌ Less customization than enterprise
BEST FOR: Small businesses 1-20 people

DIY SOLUTIONS (Free):
✅ No monthly cost
❌ Manual everything
❌ Doesn't scale
❌ Error-prone
❌ Can't automate
BEST FOR: One-person operations (temporarily)
```

**3. Why Our Mechanism is Superior (For Them)**
```
For small businesses, here's what matters most:

1. Will your team actually use it? (Simple CRM wins)
2. Can you set it up this week? (Simple CRM wins)
3. Does it pay for itself? (Simple CRM wins - $40/mo vs 10hrs saved)

Enterprise CRMs are amazing... if you have the resources.
DIY is cheap... until you calculate the time cost.

Simple CRMs hit the sweet spot:
• Easy enough to implement today
• Powerful enough to grow with you
• Affordable enough to be a no-brainer

That's why we built [Product Name] specifically for businesses like yours.
```

**4. Proof of Superior Results**
```
Companies that switched from Salesforce to us:
"Setup took 2 hours instead of 2 months. Team adoption went from 30% to 95%."

Companies that upgraded from spreadsheets:
"Stopped losing leads. Closed 40% more deals. Pays for itself 20x over."

The proof is in the results.
```

**5. Call to Action (Try Our Solution)**
```

[Start Free Trial]

[See Comparison Chart]
```

Move them from **Solution Aware → Product Aware**

## Stage 4: PRODUCT AWARE (They Know About Your Product)

### Who They Are:
- Know your product exists
- Considering buying
- Comparing you to competitors
- Looking for reasons to trust

### What They Need:
- Specific proof
- Risk reversal
- Final objection handling
- Reason to buy NOW

### Copy Approach:

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Re-explain the problem (they know)
- ❌ Re-explain the solution (they know)
- ❌ Be vague about what you offer

**DO:**
- ✅ Address specific objections
- ✅ Show detailed proof
- ✅ Compare to named competitors
- ✅ Provide strong guarantee
- ✅ Create urgency

### Example Headlines:

```
✅ "[Product Name] vs [Competitor]: The Honest Comparison"

✅ "Thinking About [Product Name]? Here Are The 5 Questions
Everyone Asks (Answered Honestly)"

✅ "Why [Product Name] Costs More Than [Cheaper Competitor]
(And Why It's Still The Better Deal)"
```

### Content Structure:

**1. Acknowledge They're Comparing**
```
You're smart to compare options.

We've been in your shoes. You want to make sure you're:
• Not overpaying
• Getting the features you need
• Working with a company that'll be around
• Making the right choice

Here's the honest truth about us vs the competition...
```

- Already bought from you (retargeting), OR
- Clicked your ad multiple times, OR
- Abandoned cart, OR
- Free trial users

### What They Need:
- Reminder
- Incentive
- Urgency
- Reason to buy NOW

### Copy Approach:

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Waste time re-selling the concept
- ❌ Educate (they already know)
- ❌ Be pushy (it backfires)

**DO:**
- ✅ Be direct and brief
- ✅ Offer incentive
- ✅ Create real urgency
- ✅ Make it stupid-easy to buy

## APPLYING THE 5 STAGES TO YOUR FUNNEL

### Stage Alignment:

```
COLD TRAFFIC (Unaware):
Platform: YouTube, TikTok, Broad Facebook
Format: Education, Stories, "Did you know..."
Goal: Problem awareness
CTA: Free content (blog, video, report)

WARM TRAFFIC (Problem/Solution Aware):
Platform: Facebook, Instagram, Google Search
Format: Comparison, Solution education
Goal: Solution preference
CTA: Free trial, Lead magnet, Webinar

HOT TRAFFIC (Product Aware):
Platform: Retargeting, Email, Direct mail
Format: Offer, Guarantee, Urgency
Goal: Purchase decision
CTA: Buy now, Limited offer

HOTTEST (Most Aware):
Platform: Email, SMS, Push notification
Format: Reminder, Incentive, Last chance
Goal: Immediate action
CTA: Complete purchase, Reactivate
```

## SCHWARTZ'S KEY INSIGHTS

### 1. Match Message to Awareness Stage
"The same copy that works for solution-aware will BOMB with unaware prospects."

### 2. You Can't Skip Stages
"You can't sell a product to someone who doesn't know they have a problem."

### 3. Most Traffic is Cold
"95% of your potential market is unaware or problem-aware. If you only write for hot traffic, you're leaving 95% of money on the table."

### 4. Sophistication Levels Matter Too
"Markets go through sophistication stages. Early market = claim the benefit. Saturated market = new mechanism required."

---

## COMMON MISTAKES

❌ **Selling to Unaware Traffic**
"Buy my weight loss pill!" to someone who doesn't think they're overweight = waste

❌ **Educating Most Aware**
Writing a 3,000-word explanation to someone who's already in their cart = lost sale

❌ **Wrong CTA for Stage**
"Buy now" to someone just discovering the problem = too soon

❌ **One-Size-Fits-All Copy**
Using the same ad for all awareness levels = poor performance
