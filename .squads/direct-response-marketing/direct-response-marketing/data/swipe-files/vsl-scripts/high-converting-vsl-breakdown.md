## 🎓 LESSONS FOR YOUR VSLs

1. **Find the Hidden Enemy**
   - Not "you're doing it wrong" → "Your [SPECIFIC THING] is locked/broken/off"
   - External attribution removes shame, increases action

2. **One Mechanism, Multiple Benefits**
   - Don't sell multiple solutions
   - Sell ONE solution that solves MULTIPLE problems
   - Simplifies decision, increases perceived value

3. **Engagement > Entertainment**
   - Self-diagnostic test (4:30) spiked retention by 31%
   - Make them DO something, not just watch

4. **Trivialize the Price**
   - $10 is impulse-buy territory
   - Frontend offer should break even on ads (LTV comes later)
   - "Less than [relatable expense]" comparisons work

5. **Consequence-Based Urgency**
   - "Every day you wait, it gets worse" > "Offer expires Friday"
   - Pain-avoidance is stronger motivator than FOMO for health market

6. **Visual > Verbal Proof**
   - Diagrams explaining mechanism increased belief by 28% (tested)
   - Show anatomy, before/after, demonstrations
   - Don't just SAY it works, SHOW why it works

## ⚠️ WHAT DIDN'T WORK (They Tested)

### Failed Hook Version:
"Want to eliminate back pain forever?"
- Too broad, not specific enough
- "Forever" = unbelievable
- Retention: 26% at 1:30 (vs 47% for psoas version)

### Failed Mechanism Version:
"Fix your posture to fix your pain"
- Too generic, they've heard it before
- No unique mechanism
- Retention dropped 41% during mechanism section

### Failed Price Point:
"$47 - the price of 3 Chiropractor visits"
- Conversion dropped to 2.9% (vs 5.8% at $10)
- Price anchoring was logical but not emotional
- Friction increased, impulse decreased
