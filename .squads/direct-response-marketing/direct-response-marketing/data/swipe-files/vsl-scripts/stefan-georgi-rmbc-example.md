### [8:45 - 12:00] THE RMBC METHOD REVEAL

```
"So I started using this process for every project.

And I refined it into a 4-step system.

I call it RMBC.

R - Research
M - Mechanism
B - Big Idea
C - Copy

Let me break it down:

---

**STEP 1: RESEARCH (50% of your time)**

This is where most people fail. They skip this or rush through it.

Big mistake.

You need to spend HALF your time here.

Here's what you're looking for:

1. Customer Language - The exact words they use to describe their pain
2. Failed Attempts - What they've tried that didn't work
3. Desired Outcome - What they want (in their words, not yours)
4. Hidden Objections - What's stopping them from buying
5. Excitement Triggers - What makes them emotional

Where to research:
• Amazon reviews (gold mine)
• Reddit/Facebook groups (unfiltered truth)
• Customer surveys (ask directly)
• Competitor reviews (what they love, what they hate)
• YouTube comments (how they talk)

You're mining for GOLD. Direct quotes. Specific complaints. Exact desires.

Take 30-40 hours minimum. Yes, hours.

This is the foundation. Skip this, fail later.

---

**STEP 2: MECHANISM (Find the 'Secret Ingredient')**

Now you analyze your research looking for the UNIQUE MECHANISM.

This is what makes your product DIFFERENT.

Not better. DIFFERENT.

Ask yourself:
• What does this product do that others don't?
• What's the 'secret ingredient' or unique approach?
• What mechanism does it use to deliver results?

Example:
• Weight loss: Not 'eat less, move more' → It's 'activate brown fat'
• Marketing: Not 'better ads' → It's 'intention-based filtering'
• Productivity: Not 'time management' → It's 'energy management'

The mechanism should be:
✅ Unique (not generic)
✅ Believable (backed by science or proof)
✅ Interesting (makes them curious)

This becomes your differentiator.

---

**STEP 3: BIG IDEA (The Transformative Philosophy)**

The Big Idea is NOT the mechanism.

It's the PHILOSOPHY that makes the mechanism matter.

It's the 'why this changes everything' framing.

Example mechanisms → big ideas:
• Mechanism: 'Activate brown fat' → Big Idea: 'Your body has a hidden fat-burning organ'
• Mechanism: 'RMBC Method' → Big Idea: 'Copy is assembled, not written'
• Mechanism: 'Grand Slam Offer' → Big Idea: 'Make price irrelevant through value'

The Big Idea should:
✅ Be counterintuitive (challenge common beliefs)
✅ Create an 'aha!' moment
✅ Be repeatable (they'll share it)

This is what they REMEMBER and TALK ABOUT.

---

**STEP 4: COPY (Assemble, Don't Invent)**

Now - and ONLY now - do you write.

But you're not 'writing.'

You're ASSEMBLING.

You take:
• The pain points from research (their exact words)
• The mechanism you identified
• The big idea framing
• The proof and testimonials

And you structure it into a proven template.

Use a VSL structure:
0:00 - Pattern interrupt
0:15 - Hook (promise + credibility)
1:00 - Story (relatable struggle)
5:00 - Mechanism reveal (your unique approach)
10:00 - Proof (testimonials, results)
15:00 - Offer (value stack)
18:00 - Guarantee (risk reversal)
20:00 - Urgency (why now)
22:00 - CTA (what to do)

You're not guessing what to say.

You're using the research to tell you EXACTLY what to say.

At each stage of the VSL, you're pulling from your research notes.

Customer language. Their pain. Their desires. Their objections.

You just organize it into the structure.

That's it.

That's the RMBC Method."
```
